Nashville Tennessee March 8th 1862 Dear wife and Beloved children i sit down this saturday night to write you a few line to let you know what i have sent you home i have bought for you all I bought the pictures of the battles of the wild cat And logans Cross Roads and Crossing fishing creek By a Rope and the seen at camp Dick at church on the 10th of November and a map with them they are rold up to gether and i want you to take good care of them untill i get home if so lucky as to do so And then i will frame them and have them many days here after in the house

Now i will just say that I have seen Daniel hartley he does not look like him self any more he is like uncle Ebb Now he has red whiskers and I heard from G. W. Byers and the rest of the Boys A. D. Hemery and John was out to see them i would like to go out to see them But we cant get out to see them they keep us very close in camp you may Tell virgenia that gilbert And the rest of the Boys was well thursday last Now i will just say that I got a letter from Mr George McCaughey yesterday And he said he had got the milerstown Drums
