February 4th 1862 Camp Denny Ky Dear wife I sit down this morrning to inform that i I am well all to the collick I have bin running since yesturday with the diareiar but it is stoped now I heard that you got pretty bad hurt be fore you got home from tom I was very sorry to hear such News so soon after i left you Joseph Bone sent a little note in a letter to James Bone to me dated 27 of last month and he said that you had just got home that day I hope you will soon get well again take good care of your self till your ribs gets well i did not hear whare you was when you got hurt whether at toms or at your

Pap or how you got hurt It stated that you was throwed from a horse and then they said that you fell off of a fence some whare i fell verry lonesome when i think of the times at home with you and the children By a good fire place and fire I have often thought of that althoug i am not tired of the service yet but the boys say if they ware foot they would not be caught so easy again We haive no idea when we will get our pay than you have i do not look it before the first of next month And then we will draw the wages for four month ANd how to get it home i know not we have no express office near us whare we are at this time i know that
