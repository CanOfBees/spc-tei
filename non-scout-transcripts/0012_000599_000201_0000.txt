UNION CAROLINE M. BROWN July 4th 1862 Tuscumbia Alabama Dear Caroline it is with much pleasure That I sit down to Answer your kind Letters of the 22 & 25. I Received them yesterday And was truly Glad to hear from you once more. It found me in good health Once more you must Excuse me for not writing To you Sooner for I wanted to know what we was going to do we Are a post guard at this Time at this place but How long we will Stay

Page 2

Know not we may Stay Along time we have One of the best Springs That Ever come out of The ground it would water The hole Earth full of People if they ware here It comes out of the Ground about 30 feet wide and it boils up And runs so swift that No man can wade it And just above this One the Say there is Another one that is three Times as big as this I have not bin to see It I know that it Makes quite a Creek Whare we are and as Clear as well water and Very cold I was in it

To wash off yesterday I tell you it was very Cold Now i will tel you about the lay of The Country it is a very Nice laing Contry much Better than what I saw Of Mississippi and they Have a large Crop of Corn for the rebels to Live on and carry on The war they are not Raising any Cane this Year Either Some of the Citizen told me that They aloud one man Out of ten in texes To raise cane for home use

you may Exspect to pay for the Shugar that we Get after this war for Some time I will just Say that I in tend to Try to get a furlow when we get our next pay And come home to See you we wont have any way to Send our money Home and if we keep It here we will Spend It for a great many Things that could do with out and so I thought I had as well Spend it To come home to See you for i want to See you all the worst old way and the old place If it is rought a home So good by HENRY BROWN
