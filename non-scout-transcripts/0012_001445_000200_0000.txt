Nashville March the 20 1874 My ever Dear Sister Electa I no you cannot think me very Dear in not writing, to you, but alass I cannot frame no excuse only negligence I cannot rember the last letter I rec from you but I believe it was about the time Uncle Ben David Died I do not remember wheather I have answered it or not, with me time flies fast that Is bearing me to my long home, but I do not wish to complain, nor wish my time long or short but hope to be resined to the will of God in all things, the Lord is & has been very good unto me all the days of my life, therefore would I desire to be thanful myhealth is & has been very good through the Summer & fall during the Chollory times I went up in the country on the Farm & stayed some 3 or 4 weeks it was a very distressing & alarming time all that could & was able to get a way left for the country & wattering places I cannot paint the seen to you but as I live close by 2 underlakes I had a great chance to see of its awafull havoc one day it was published that there was 1,27 died in one day & doubtless that was not all every one felt the efect more or less some took provisions & went to the woods as they could not find accomodations buisness of all kinds was suspeneded, it is called now healthy as is common, but times are called very hard & dull in the Months of May I was more the Buisey from Sept to Christmas

I have kept 2 girls have one with me now a going to school we have had very interesting meetings this Winter I have enjoyed them the best I ever have since I have been hear Prayer Meetings hear are not conducted as they are where I used to be few are called onto Pray no one says anything but the Preacher, my past life more more then surpriseth me, I have no desire to live my life over again sometimes I rejoice that my days are so fast number my up then a gain I fear that I like Moses may never be permitted to enter Canaan the Promised land but God hat sed come unto me & be ye saved Dear Sister how is it with you are you ready waiting & prepared for the coming of the Son of Man we according to the corse of nature have not many years longer to live let us try & live so we may meat each other in a better wourld - we have had a very pleasant winter no snow but once some got out these steds for sport but you could hear the grit & gridn so they did not want not maney Bells the Peach trees are now in Bloom but I fear they may be frosts & inguse them we are now having much rain I dont think this is much of a Farming country or it is subject to eather wet or dry but still the country is kept up still flourishes & populates both with white & Black, I wish you could come & see me I would take all the pains I could in trying to show you the Southern Stile, but that would
