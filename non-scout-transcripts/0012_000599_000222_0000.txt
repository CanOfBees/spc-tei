February 2th 1862 Dear wife and children I sit down to let you know how I am at this time I am well at this time and hope these few lines will find you all the same we crost the Cumberland River last Friday in a little boat and the boys puld by a rope to cross we are one mile and a half from the river on a hill There are some good union Men here and we cant tell when we will move from this place some say we will go down to dixy but they cant tell any better than i can But they are willing to Any place they like to be moveing onward one

way or the other they do not want to stay too long at one place our boys say they will have meat They went out yesturday and fetched in two hogs and We are livingn well to day you may bet plenty of pork now the boys goes for hogs geese ducks and chickens and honey They have any thing they want And we intend to have anough to eat and drink as we go Down to Dixy i want you to send the news to me about the weather up there in Ohio whethe it is cold or not we have not had any Frost down here since i got back we have had rain prty near every day since i got back to camp anough of that

Now I will just say that i maid a miss stake I sending your letters to Millerstown I sent you a newspaper to hartleyville And i want you to write word to me whethe you got it or not i have a little loard on my neet about a fat square but we must do the best we can here in camp we will hafter to go on dress perrade soon I shall have to quite writeing I feel very lonsome here since i got back to camp i think a grate deal about home i hope god will you and children and myself to i would like to see you all the best kind But i shall not have the previelige any more
