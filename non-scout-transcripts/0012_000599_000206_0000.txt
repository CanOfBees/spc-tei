September 11th 62 Convalesent Camp CAROLINE M. BROWN Near Nashville Tennessee Dear wife it is with regret that I seat myself To inform you whare I am I am in the camp Above mentioned about One mile from Nashville Waiting my discharge and we that are here are used more like dogs than men we only get one third Rashons and that is not a Nought we have to buy Some Bread and potatoes and a little meat to keep us along i think we will be Sent to Louisville Before Long and then home

Good thing you have laid up for me you must Not make your Self Unesy about me and you Must Excuse me for not writing before this because I have bin moved to 3 Difernt places since i rote The last letter to you And now i cant advis you To write to me for i cant Tell what day we may Be moved from this camp To Some other so i shall Say for you not to write untill i write again to To you if we are moved To Louisville i shall come Home Shure my feet and Legs are not sweld so Bad as they ware some time ago but

So good By to you all I cant walk to do any Good and dont know As i Ever shall any more N. B. John S. Hemery Got wounded in the Thigh last mondy they ware going out on a Scout and the out line Of our pickets fired on Them and wounded Him now i will just Say that i will be at Home as soon as i can Get there you may bet my legs achees while writeing these few Lines there is 6 of co A waiting for their Papers myself and R Scrivoner and John McClurg and the other I need not mention HENRY BROWN.
