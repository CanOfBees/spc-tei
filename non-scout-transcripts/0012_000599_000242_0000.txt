Camp Near Corrinth Miss May 30th 62 Caroline M. Brown Dear Companion it is with much pleasure that i sit down this Saturday morning to let you know how I am I feel much better than when i wrote to you before i feel week yet but i am able for duty i hope these few lines will find you all well when it comes to hand you wanted to know if warner spurrier was dead is not he is camp with us And is harty as ever i gess there is a grate many of our boys left behind and have not come of

little colt for me to see with the regt yet they are kep as cooks henry Strate John McNeal Stephen Hemery Jo Hammon I N Joseph and some more is at Nashville yet i think there is about one half of our company left behind And gone home i was out out on picket Night be fore last and sent out some skurmishers and they got to fighting a little they maid the bullets whistle over our heads you had better think for a while we are with in one mile of the rebels Breast work and looking for a fight all the time and we all wish for it to go off so we can

I hope you will leave a nice get home we have poor watter whare we are now Nothing but swamp watter And of the worst kind at that we are well fortifide wheare we are man with good breast works I think it wont be long till we will be i corrinth we heard verry heavy cannon adeing in that way this morning we have not heard what they done the ashes came fling over and camp some pierces was caught we could not read it we supose part of the town was set on fire with bums

To the boys good little fellows Alfred hemery was down our line the other day some four miles and And saw ike groves and Tom Martin the rest of the Groves he did not see yes he saw Josh he says he is as big as vest Elias died since he left home I have got too letters from Indiana one dated the 17 and they ware all well then one from Bob and the other from Caroline I gave dick hartley some paper the other day he was up to see us to write you a letter Excuse my writing i shall close I am so nerse write soon good by Henry Brown
