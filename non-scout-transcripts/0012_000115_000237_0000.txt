Warrenton Sept 28. 1833 Dear Burford Yours of the 28th inst was duly rcd: giving me the gratifying intelegence of your promotion & the happy result of the long & vexations Land suit - Subsequent to the date of my last letter to you, I have added about 20 more of the right sort of stock to my number, making in all on hand 60, now on the road & ready for market - We are all in high spirits, and nothing but misfortune will prevent our making all sorts of money this year provided we are not caught by some sudden hangition in the cotton market men $600 all other in the same proportion

Page 3

I thank you for the Banner, for it always affords me the pleasure to see the news direct from Nashville - I had the pleasure of shaking hands with our esteemed friend Col Allen a few days since, on his return from the East - We are looking out for hard times in the Money Market on account of the anticipated removal of the government diposits The small fry are quaking - For my part I long in the old cocks inflexible determination to rid the country of a growing evil - Yr Friend JMSaunders
