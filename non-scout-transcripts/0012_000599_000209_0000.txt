DEATH TO TRAITORS Camp Clark September 19th 1861 Dear Companion I take this time to inform you that I am well at this time and I hope these few lines may find you the same and all by the Rest of the folks special the children and phene I have not found home since I left there but we have better fare here than we did at Camp Chase the folks are very cover to us here. It is a very pretty place for to camp they want our Reg to camp here all winter and guard the city for them

I have sent my cloth to the Maines and i want you to go or send up and get them home as soon as you can for they were Damp wferd i put them in the sack. Mr Hattan and Josephys are in the box and Geo Gavers to they have got their name on the bundle you will find two pictures in my coat pocket in the side pocket the only will know and the other is John McNeal on a plate and he wants you to send it to his mother and tell them that he is all rite he has gone up to graly virginia on

a boat Caroline Lewis is here to day and Slallson and wife and old Sammy Hook Perry Shanner has Bin sick for some and I dont think he will over to night he has been out of his head for two days and nights I have averry poor place to writ on a seat of a wagon and a shorttint that I have know idea where we will go from hear on less we go to virginia I want you to write soon and let me know how you all are and the Neighbours in genera tell the boys that i will send them something after a while when I get some money

I want to send better clothes to B. Norris as soon as you get them home and george gavers to I want you to send me word how McCaughly is a getting long with his company My love to you all. Husband and Father and Friend Just tell me how you are a getting along Henry Brown
