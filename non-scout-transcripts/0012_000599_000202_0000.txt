July 15th 1862 Camp Near Tuscumbia CAROLINE M. BROWN Most Dear wife I Sit down this nice morning to let you know how i am This Morning my Health is not So good as It was when i wrote to You last my feet is very much Sweld again the Hurt me this time very much they look as though They would burst open It may be they will Send me home as Soon as we Are paid of again we are Looking for the pay master On Every day now To pay us we will be

Page 2

Paid against the first of next month any way i do hope These fewe lines will find You in good health at least Better than my health is At this time it has bin Some time Since i got a Letter from you we have Had 2 or 3 mails since i Got any from you the Other boys gets letters From Hartley ville and me None i do not write as Often as Some of the Boys for i do not feel Like writeing when i Am not well but i want you to write as Often as you can you Have a good chance to Write Every Sunday If you want to do so

Page 3

I think i have Answerd All your letters and more To our regt went out to Ruselsville last Saturday And have not returnd yet We heard that they would Be back to morrow morning They went out on a scout we heard that thearewas A squad of rebels out in The mountains but they Could not find them So we heard this morning John Hemery has bin quite Sick but he is better now He has bin at the hospicalle Ever since we have bin At this place he is here To day cooking Some Black berries but very weak the rest of the Boys are mostly well

Page 4

Henry Strait are with us again he is not very well he has The yellow jondice he is at his quarters he Is not confined to his Bed but is not able For duty at present now I have never said much About being tired of The Servise but i have Thought agreat deal about It and i will omit Telling my thoughts for Awhile yet but it is not As i Exspected by along ways at any rate you may know that by the way i write to you this time i love the verri old house you Stand in So good by HENRY BROWN.
