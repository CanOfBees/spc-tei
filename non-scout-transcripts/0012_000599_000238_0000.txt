April 15th 1862 Dear Wife and Children i sit down this morning To let you know that i Am well This morning after A march of about 40 miles Whitch finds us within One mile of the tennessee River near Clifton a little Town on the The River and About 40 miles from the Battle ground whare they Fought last Sunday and monday a week ago they say our loss was 4,000 And theres loss was

Page 2

much more the Battle Fought near Savanah on the other side of the River we are more careful on a nice piece of ground But how long we will Stay Here we no not we may have To fighting to do yet the Rebels are within 30 miles of whare we are now we Heard That the 18th regment was in ut and that they ware but all to pieces but we Hope it is not so they say they are not done burriing The dead yet so you may Judge for your Self what evas killed we have heard that The rebels have left Richmond we hope it is so if it is so I thind that the war will soon Be over it has lasted much

longer than i though it would when i was at home so i will say more about it Now that is anougth of That for this time I still feel like we will Get home Safe again if we keep will we have more a death once and A while we heard that Garrer and aburn was At home and getting Better aure sick boys are at Nashville J Hemery Jr Imernea And J. N. Joseph was better But it will be some time before they can over take us it has not bin nerry warm yet we have had cloud weathy for some time

Now I feel for your wellfare i know that i ought to be at home with you and the boys now but i cannot at this time know i will Have to clos we have to Go on drill sonn Direct your better to Clifton Tennessee now you must Do the best you if i ever get home from this war it will be a smart man will get me in again you may bet your Soul i have To Shed tears every letters I write and Every one I get from you all tho we Are so far apart no more At present but remain you truly until death write as soon as you get this Henry Brown
