United States of America. This Indenture, made the 18 day of November in the year of our Lord 1794 between John Sevier chairman of the county court, of the county of Washington and Territory aforesaid, on behalf of the justices of the said county, and their successors, of the one part, and Andrew Carson of the other part, Witnesseth, That the said John Sevier in pursuance to an order of said county court, made the 18 day of November and acording to the directions of the Act of Assembly in the case made and provided, doth put, place and bind unto the said Andrew Carson George Stout an Orphan, now of the age of 9 years with the said Andre Carson to live after the manner of an apprentice and servant, until the said apprentice shall attain the age of 21 years: During all which time the said apprentice his master shall faithfully serve, his lawful commands every where gladly obey; he shall not at any time absent himself from his said masters service without leave, but in all things as a good and faithful servant shall behave towards his said master and the said Andrew Carson doth covenant, promise, and agree, to and with the said George Stout to learn the art of making whels and that will constantly find and provide for said apprentice during the term aforesaid, sufficient diet, washing, lodging, and apparel, fiting for an apprentice; and also all other things necessary, both in sickness and in health. In witness whereof, the parties to these presents, have interchangeably set their hands and seals, the day and year first written above. John Sevier CW Sea Test John Sevier CC Andrew Carson Sea also to learn the said apprentice to read write and cypher as far as the Single rule of three and a full set of whell rights tools and five dollars in cash
