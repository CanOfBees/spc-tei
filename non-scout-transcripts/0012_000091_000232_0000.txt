Cicero , Ind July the 16th/65 Dear brother I again take my pen in hand to write you a few lines to let you know that I have not forgot you yet we are all well as common and I cincereley hope if this reaches your kind hand it will find you blessed with good health, we received you letter of the ninth we was glad to hear from you once more but we was sorrow to hear that you did not get enuf to eat if you was at home I could get you something to ear if i had a bin you boys I would a shot that officer the fourth of July

why dont you all write a letter and send it to President Johnson and tell him how they starve you. I hope you will get to come home soon now I must tell you who is coming here to day Aunt Alanda and David Gasber John Stokes and John Whisler I wish you could be at home we would have a good time Some of our apples is getting good pap has not got our wheat all stacked yet him and Nervill made one rich a friday Green us up here ever day nearly now I hardley know what to write that will interest you if you was at home I would tell you more than I can write it is only 4 weeks till conference

I must bring my letter to a close you must excuse bad writing and spelling for this was wrote in a hurry Sanks folks is well and all the rest of the folks a for as I know write soon as you can yours until death Sallie Slack To Albert Slack give my best respects to all of the boys
