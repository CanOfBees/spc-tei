October 3th 1861 Camp Dick Robinson Kentucky, Gerard County Dear wife & Children i take this time to inform you that i am at this time hopeing these few lins may find you the same we are now in an Enemy land 1.30 miles From Cinnatti out in Kentucky and i exspect we will leave here in a week or sooner for Tennessee wee supose that will Be the point I would like to see you all verry much it may be the Last time that i will have the Chance to write I nominate aly

So Would of sent you all A present we got None and there is no Prosspect of any now so Far from home it is a verry pretty place who we have Campt and all of Kentucky that we have past througt from The River and Seeder Bushes plenty along The road NB the darkeys are neumerous Througt here and white Folks scarse i dont want you to fret about me if you can help it for i shall do the best i Can for my self and our Captain will for us you bet

we saw about three thousands People in the City on Sunday Before we left some 2 or 3 hundred women the Union men are verry Close to us here them That are union and Right we Campt in An open field tuesday Nigh Seces at that we tore a straw pile all to peces No more At present But Remain yours for ever Henry Brown
