This deed made the twenty sixth day of October, anno domini, eighteen hundred and thirty seven, between William Payne of the one part and David Burford of the other part, both of Smith County, witnesseth that for and in consideration of the sum of ten dollars by the said David to the said William in hand paid and for and in consideration of the uses and trusts here in after mentioned, the Lord William Payne hath granted, bargaind, sold transferred and conveyed to the said David Burford the following real and personal estate, trust, one tract of land lying in said County, containing two hundred and twenty two acres, more or less, adjoining the lands of Mrs Hammock ,Thomas Willbourn and others, part of said tract being the same conveyed to said William Payne by William Parker, as [smudge] residence of said tract being the same conveyed to said William Payne by Richard Parker and Thomas Willbourne - and reference is to be had to said deeds for the boundaries of said tract - said tract of land is the same on which the said William Payne now resides. Also one negro man slave, named Bird, of dark colour aged twenty five years - one negro woman slaved named Patty of yellow colour, aged thirty years - one negro boy slave named Joe, of yellow colour aged sixteen years - one negro boy slave named Cephus, of dark colour, aged fifteen years - one negro girl slave named Mary, of dark colour, aged six years - one negro girl slave named Letha of dark colour, aged five years - One negro boy slave named Lawson, of yellow colour aged eighteen months - One negro woman slave named Charlotte of dark colour, aged thirtyfive years - one negro girl slave

To have and to hold said tract of land, and said ten negro slaves with their future increase, to the said David Burford, his heirs and assigns forever against the claim of the said William Payne and all other persons whatsoever. It is agreed and declared that said David Burford shall give ten days notice of all sales to be made by him, by advertisement in writing at the Parish, and also in Carthage - and that the sales shall be for cash, and by public auction - provided however, said sales or either of them may be by private contract, the said William Payne consenting and joining in the deed or bill of sale with said trustee, and the process of said sale being by said trustee applied to the payment of said notes. This further agreed and declared that said William Payne may continued in possession of said land and also of the negroes, until the same may be called for by said David Burford for the purpose of making sales as herein decided. Unless, in the opinion of said trustee, said property or any part thereof shall be in danger of being removed, mal treated, or the safety of said Corley & Johnsons claims otherwise endangered - Then or in within of said courts, it is agreed and declared that said David Burford shall take said property into his possession, and appoint such receiver, manager or overseer as he may deem necessary - should the [smudge] the debts herein mentioned be paid by said William & Hamilton, without a sale of said property, or any part thereof, then, said David Burford is to recovery the same, to said William Payne, at his the said Payees, cost and charges - so, any surplus of said property or the proceeds thereof, after satisfying the debts herein mentioned, is to be conveyed, or paid over, to said William Payne. All cost and charges incurred by said David Burford in the execution of the within trusts to be paid out of the estate conveyed or by the said William Payne.
