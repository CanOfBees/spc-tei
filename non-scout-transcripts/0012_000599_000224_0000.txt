February 12th 1862 Dear Companion I seat my self to let you know how i am at this time I am well and i Hope these few lines will Find you and the boys the enjoying the same blessing i will just say that we are on our way Back to lebannon we are 2 miles this side of Crab orchard And we will reach lebannon to Night within 18 miles of Danville we left the camp on the other side of the river last friday And crost the river on saturday and cmpt over sunday and this is the third day we have bin marching 33 miles you

May guess we do not go verry fast althto we had very bad roads untill we got Crab orchard and we then lue got on the pike I supose we will go to green River soon and then i exspect we will have A little fight to do when we get there with old Buckner or Breckinridge one or the other down there the health of the reg is tolable good at this time there is but verry few of the boys but what is able to walk by them halling their knapsack the road we have to travel is very good on to lebannon it is a pike clere through

I will just say that i saw some of the homeliest woman down in whare we have bin that i ever saw i think they are boath dirty and ragged and not verry smart and the poorest houses you ever saw our house is good to what the most of them are down thare they Are boath little and rough you may tell Virgenia Byers that i got a letter G.H. Byers that was dated the 30 of last month stateing that he was well at that time he said that the Health of their regt was not As good as he would like it to be i got a letter from Catharine green stateing that
