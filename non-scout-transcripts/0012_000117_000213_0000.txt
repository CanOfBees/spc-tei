Mail New Orleans, March 23rd 1853 David Burford Esq Dixons Springs Tennessee Dear Sir, We received a day or two since yours of got 2nd (postmarked 11th) requesting us to invest in the amount sent in by Mister Wharton - for 3 or 4 March - There is demand for money but have Out is a rather delicate matter. - We have thought Over it every way and have concluded to invest it by purchasing @ 8% pannum discount the paper named below leaving a little odd balance which we can get back when we collect the note. - The Bank rates for any loan under 4 Mos is limited to 6% and they do all the good (first class) paper they can get. If you do not think proper to approve the investment we have made we will allow you Six percent per annum from this date on the amount at your Credit and take the note to Our Own account - and in this Case you Can draw for the money at your pleasure.- The note is drawn by Jos Littlejohn & Geo W Manson of this City and endorsed by themselves dated 19 June 1850 - payable One year of the date say /22 June 1833 at Louisiana State bank secured by Mortgage on City real estate and bearing interest at 6% pum from date to maturity and after maturity if not then paid it bears 8% pum Amount of not - $1.350.- 1 yr Ins to 19/22 June 53 (6%) 87.- $1.431 discount 3 Mos. Ins @ 8% pum 28.62 $1,402.38 to your debit Fourteen hundred and two 38/100 Dollars - Please advise us promptly whether you wil approve this or leave the amount of your Credit subject to your Check @6% - Your Pickett, Perkins & C The following text is a continuation of the second page of the letter. P.S. Very good City acceptances due on next season can be bought at 10 per cent pannum discount.
