Convalesent Camp Near Nashville Tenn September CAROLINE M. BROWN 14th 1862 Dear Companion I Sit down This Sabath After noon To inform You how I am at this Time I am not verry well I tell you how I feel verry poorly With what i Sufer with My feet and legs and The athsma has set in with the rest or Something Like it i have to coff A grate deal at night And Some throught The day and i am Getting verry poor

Page 2

And for getting home i See But a Slim chance For my part the old Papers are all lost and I cant tell when they will get up any new Ones for us thar is one Thing i know and is This if i was at home I would be better cared For than whare i am My feet and legs are Not weld as much as They ware Some time Ago But pain me By Spells rite Smarte The Skin just above The ancle Was allmost Growed to the bone it Feels like grisle Now If this to hand I do not want you to greave

Page 3

About my Situation For we are too far apart To help Each other at this Time altho i would Like to be with you And the rest of the Family to in Joy peace And comfort once more To gether i think if i Was there now i would Not be Excited gain For i see i cant stand Any longer in the Servise I am wore out with Hardships and Exsposier I do hope these few Lines will find you all In good health I will Just we have not bin Paid any Since i sent you them 12 Dollars there Is about Sixty dollars

Page 4

Due me now if i had Some of that now i Could get thing that i Could Eat you wanted To know whare you Should Sow Some wheat If i did not get home In time you had better Try and get the corn Cut up as soon as it will do atall and Sow that field that A Svers had in and the Orchard lot up as far As that row of peach Trees back of the house you must do the best you can and Cook Look for me untill you see me comeing No more yours untill Death good by HENRY BROWN.
