Caroline M. Brown Camp Near Corinth Mississippi 62 June 13th Dear and most beloved wife Caroline i received yours of the 3d and was glad to hear from you once more it found me well all but my feet they still keep sweld yet I cant ware my boots atll I am not able for duty on the account of them I cant write much news i gave you the new in the other letter before this i can tell you whare we are at this time we are about one mile from Corinth

Page 2

And i exspect we will stay here some time for general Thomas have bin apointed goner of miss as a military man and we have to guard the place for some time and we hope we will be sent home from this point we have our camp in an open field and you had better guess it is hot we still have plenty to eat such as it is Now i will tel you a little more About my self some says that i am getting the dropsy the doctor cant tell what is the matter with me

Page 3

I dont know but what I will try to get a discharde if i do not get better soon or a forlough and come home and stay a while with you and see if doctor Holden cant do me some good i think he can beat a field full of our doctors and not half try as mach Noiris you said the wheat look pretty well i wished I was thare to see it crit If i did not do any myself you also said your corn was fit to work and the bees had swarmed twise And that you had sold Wm Law four sheep At two dollars and 25 cts per head i dont know But i had better stay away

A. D. Hemery is a going to try to get a forlough to come home about the twentieth of this mont And i would like to come along with him If i could but i am afraid neither one of us will get to come that the matter with hannas I do not want you to bild to strong on this for fear it will not work out rite to please us all so well you said that Mrs Law wanted me to come home in peach time so she could come up and hear me tell some big lies about my travls so good by on thy Henry Brown
