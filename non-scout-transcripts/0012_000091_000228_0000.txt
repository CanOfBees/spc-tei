Camp Curtin Feb 20 th / 65 Dear Anna Monday after dinner i sit down in my tent to write you a few lines in hope that they may receive them enjoying your usual health as i do through the mercy of God. I shall give you a little account of our journey and the kind of company I had There was 2 guards on each car there was about 28 men in each car and they were the roughest and the vulgarest set i ever saw in my life when we would stop at a station they would even insult women that were paying by nothing but cursing swearing and Blaspheming all the time i never was with such an ungodly crew before in my life one of the guards told me that he seen great many squads within the last three years but he never saw the like of them fellows as soon as they got to Harrisburg they got drunk and had to be put in the Guard Room so they were robbed there of all the money they had pretty much all their

the first rations to we draw a loaf in morning about the size of your 5 cent loaf also a tin of Coffee dinner we get some bean soup and a Piece of Meat to might it will be a cup of hot coffee then to beds at 8 o clock Camp Curtin has 200 tents besides the officers tents and other Buildings each tent is calculated to accommodate 6 men we are but 5 in ours though I dont know of anything else to write just now Yesterday was my birthday Tell Mr Holmes that i took some of his snuff while i was wanting I have a Piece of that cake yet and 3 of the apples I have got the carpet sack here some of the fellows lost their knapsack and all they have but I took care of all mine where I wont i took all along with me I did not know that you had put the 45$ in my pocket book till yesterday when I was hunting after the Letter stamps i happen to see them but i suppose that you was confused and did not do it intentionally I have not spent a single cent yet
