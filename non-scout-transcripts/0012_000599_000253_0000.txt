April 7th 1862 April 7th 1862 Ambre April May the E Mrs Caroline M. Brown Dear Wife Sit down this nice morning to Let you know that I am well at this Hopeing these few lines will find you And The Children the same We Still remain in the same camp and we do not know how long we will Stay we may Have to move Soon and we may not we know of no Rebel force any ways close Here Some down in Alabama But i do not think that we will ever See one of them Altho we may before we get home

Page 2

we are getting very lonesome we have not any letters For Some Time i got yours Of the 23 with a few lines in It from James Henry i answer Them rite away but you are Behind Several Letters yet His makes fourteen for me Since i got Back to camp And only 4 from you yet i want you to write to me Once a week while i am in The survice i think we will Get home after a while There are a great many diferent opinions about the close of This war with us in camp Some thinks it will Soon Be over and Some says that It will last a good while yet we cannot tell any thing about for we do not know

Page 3

And we dont care as long As we keep well and can Hear from home and while you Keep well Now we cant tell you any news for we cant Get much here I would like If you would Subscribe for The Perry County paper printed At New Lexington for you will get more news that I Can write there is a grate many Letters thave to be publish by The Regment you mite get the mail boy to leave them at Mrs Starshall have them Address to millesst you could get H. Ketchany to speek To the mail boy to carry Them to marshall for you Take them for Six months we have bin buying Some Types the most of Co A boys

Page 4

I bought your name and As you will See by this Letter the two cost me 75 cts I will send you two invelope with my name on them To send back to me and i want Some more Stamps Sent to me for we cannot Get any here Direct your Letters to Camp Columbia Tennessee I Shall close by Saying i would like to be at Home with you so good by I still remain your true Husband until death Boys i would like to see you very much But i Cant have the privelige yet so no more at present But Still remain your Father until death good by to all Henry Brown
