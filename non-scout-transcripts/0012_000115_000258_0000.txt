Agreement made and entered into this 25th day of October 1827 between David Burford & Jourdan M. Saunders, both of Smith County & State of Tennessee, Witnesseth, that each of said parties agree to put in two thousand dollars in current money of the U States as stock, or capital in the negro speculation business, and, that the one who makes the purchase of negroes Shall keep a true and correct account of the purchase of each negro, or lot of negroes; also an account of clothing for said negroes, traveling expences as the purchaser may think right; - also keep an account of his own traveling and other expenses while engaged on business oppertaining to said trading; also an account for hire of assistant, should it be nessesary. all of which accounts shall be paid by the firm, or acct of the joint fund, and, after paying said expenses and refunding to each their respective Sums advanced, then the balance Shall be equally divided between the two share and share alike. - And it is further understood and agreed that neither of the contracting parties are to charge any thing for their Services individually. - And it is further understoof that should either of the parties die before the Sale of any or the whole negroes belonging to them Jointly, or before the business is closed that the surviving partner is to take charge of and close the business of said firm - The name and Stile of said firm Shall be J, M, Saunders & Co And lastly it is understood that said Saunders is to take charge of the funds &make the first purchase, and finally, that this partnership is to exist until one or the other, or both, wish a discontinuance of the business. Witness our hands & Seals the day and year above written. David Burford Seal (Signed duplicates) JM Saunders Seal
