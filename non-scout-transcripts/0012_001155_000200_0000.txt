Knoxville March 10th When I last wrote you I expected to send my next Epistte by Harriet but her leaving this city is now as far in the future as it then was for aught I know She now thinks of leaving here in one week from tomorrow or in terms parlance tomorrow - week in company with a Mr. Moppett of New Market an acquantance of ours. But there is a Yankee friend of mine who l eaves here the first of may whom she would prefer going with and she is half decided to stop and go with him. I think I shall persuade her to stop as if will he far from pleasant for her to leave the Sunny South in this beautiful weather which we are having and go to the regions of ice and snow She could not go out at all now there and would have to crouch over some bodys stover from morning until night to keep from freezing after spending three winters here. I she goes next week she will stop with her Aunt Lydia Patten at Wheater Mass a short time then go to Norway and Weld before Sidney so that anyone will not be at Sidney before May- The weather here for the last few weeks is just as fine as it well can be. may flowers

and fruit trees are now in full bloom. We commenced apr Friday last the Summer is slow at the Institute with encouraging prospects A good number of pupils and bright looking ones too the same teachers will continue through this session which will close in July - The Institute is delightfully situated fronting Main Street on one side the river on the opposite and being very high the breezes are very fine - Knoxville is all excitement just now in talking of making the telegraphic mirs come on up here wich stop now in North Georgia where the railroad does - through that now does cross the Yeml line and comes to the river and would have come to this place ere now if they had a letter now than Duff Green at the head of the railroad company - He comes up here and spouts to the people occasionally on the advantages of the railroads in general and this one in particular but he is a bombastic, visionary man always building castles in the air instead of railroads on the ground - I heard by the by that the Moores of Waterville were coming out here this winter to put some of their boats on this river. There are a great many boats on the river but still the trade is great and would probably support more -

I weigh now 15 pounds more than I did when I cam here then 126 now 141 and really find it quite burdensome to carry about such a heavy body - received a letter from Edward and Mother abotu two weeks ago - I have been writing a long letter this evening and am very tired of writing besides Miss Me is reading about and talking all the time so that my ideas are getting confused and I will make this a short Epistte- I have not yet concluded who to direct this Epistte too thought you would be expecting something as it now nearly four weeks since I wrote home and guess that I will direct it to the head of the family E S Merrill You some of you said in your letters that Marie had not written home for a long time I do not see why I had a letter from her last week She the Dr and Patty are all well I do not think she was glad to see Olgi she only mentions occasionally that he is there and that he is at work at his trade and I sincerely hope that he will stay there at least that his next move will not be here. If he does come here I fear I should go into one of Jack Downing comppion fits Nahby is a wonderfully precious child according to her parents account of her
