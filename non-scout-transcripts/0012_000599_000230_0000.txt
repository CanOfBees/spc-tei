Camp Near Nashville March 18th 1862 Dear wife it is with much pleasure that i set down to inform you that i am well at time and do sincearly hope these few lines may find you And the children in good health and the friends the same we are still At this place and we do not know how long we will have to stay here or how soon we will have to here or whare we will go when we leave this place i am out of heart I have not had but one

letter from you since i left home the last time I feel as tho you have forgoton me I have nothing new to writ you for we can Not get out side of the guard lines when we want to All we here is in the Camp we still can hear good news on our side at Every side of us the word is that our men has made A great capture in missori they took one million of Dollars worth of property At that place i think the war will soon be over And we will get home soon again they say our mail was capturday to day by the Rebels if so

we will not here from one other much more i supose Altho the Hemery got some letters yesturday they said that Stephen Beal was not exspected live they did not say what was the matter with him the health of the regt is midling good at this i would just that garver and McNeal is A getting Better general Sheef bigade is together yet there is about 3000 men able for duty at this time in this bigade it is getting quite warm down here the plum trees are in blosome and some of the peach trees Are allmost out in blosome here to

Now i will just say that i have sent ten dollars with J. A. Lumm to lexington by exspress he will send it to you when he get it I exspect we will soon Again and i shall send some more home i if can Now shall clos these i would like to hear from you once more if it could be so and that soon to i have no letter stamps i will have to send them with out paying for them or pay 5 cts on each letters they have no copers here so good by for this time H, Brown
