Camp Near Somerset January 28th 1862 Dear Companion I seat my self in our tent to inform you that I feel very well at this time I was out on garde last night round the camp we moved yesterday 3 miles Below Somerset I was out to see if i could get some apple And i could not find any I forgot to tell you that Conner has got Back to camp again He was taken down to tennessee And he croled out from the Guards after night and maid his way back to camp withi much joy to the Boys of Company D I tell you we exspect to go down South after a while but we dont know how soon we will wait here till we get our pay

Page 2

The boys have bin lmg about the pay they have not received one cent yet They will all lie swear and play cards to boot I hope I shall never learn to play for i cant see any sport in it i would rather lay down and sleep Now i will state to you that i do not want you to be uneasy about me while we are here there is no danger Here know there is no rebels closer than Bolingreen as we know of now we have a bout 29 regments within 8 or 10 miles of us now I wrote Tom and McClurg last sunday tell the neighbor that I send my best respect to them general tell Mr Strait that Henry is well

Page 3

He is out teaming we have But one or two boys in the Hospittle S. Shilling and the Drumer B. Curry Cap is not verry well at this time David Condon is so fat that he cant see the way i sit is very tiresome and I shall soon close we have Drawed New tents and they are much beter than the others they will hold twenty very comfortable they are very high I will hafter to quit I want you to keep enough money when i sent it home to do you untill no more at present but still remain your husband Henry Brown Direct to Somerset yet

To my dear boys I want you to obey your mother and I will like you the Better we have to go and drill Good by Boys
