August 13th 62 Camp Near Elk River Tennessee CAROLINE M. BROWN Our Flag and Our Country! Dear Caroline I received one of your old Letters This morning and Was glad that it came to Hand altho I had one of A later date from you I Read with intrust and Saw you had bin on A visit up to rush creek And you Stated that you Had got your likeness Taken and would Send It to me if i wanted it I will just Say that I do hope to See your Face Before long for I

Page 2

Have not bin able for Duty for a grate while My feet is not Swelld So bad as they ware Some time ago but they Are still too big yet I thought i would write A few lines to you by Leutenant Primrose He Starts for home in The morning on a Furlough for his health I do not feel much like writing i will Say That we go for Every thing we want Corn Chickens Turkeys geese Sheep hogs Cattle horses mules and Negroes and honey We just sweep the Every thing before us you had better

Page 3

Think you Stated in you Last letter that you Had Sold all the Sheep But Seven lambs if I get home you will Have to by one or two Good ones for me mutton Is all I crave in the meet line and once And a while Some Fride Chicken and A little frest pork And a little Cabage And Some unions And Some potatoes I have 10 cents for one Dozen of them and not verry big at that I hope you will Have plenty of all Them things for me when i come home

Page 4

If i get to Come home Any ways soon i hope you will have Some good pickels for me to Eat and melons and Some nice Chickens To fry and plenty of Eggs and ham for we Do not get much Of that here we get good bacon and Pork that is sides And shoulders And Crackers asusea Do not look for me until you see me Give my Love To all the friends And take Some your Self Goodby To Caroline by HENRY BROWN.
