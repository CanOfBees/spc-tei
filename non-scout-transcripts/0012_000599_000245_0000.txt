June 13th 1862 Camp Near Corinth Caroline M. Brown Most respected wife I thought I would tell you about some of the boys i will tell no names but there is some of them that I thought a great deal of till went back from home there is one that we thought was verry smart that is one of the meanest and the most vulgar talking young men that i ever heard in my and he is as deceatful as the

old devel wants him to be you may guess who it is if you can he is a pretty good coquelt to now you mind if he aint just as i tell you he is agoing to make a fool of somebody if they dont wach that is anough of that you may till Mrs Law that i think i will be at home against peach time so she can hear tell something about the swamps down here for i tell you there is anough of them here said that you had not got the letter that I wrote to the Boys you will get it yet so good by to you

A few words to the boys Now boys we are a great ways apart you are in the state of Ohio and i am in the Confederate State of Mississippi a grate ways apart now i am a fighting for your liberty as well as mine now I want you to do the best you can for me at home and let the neighbours see what good boys you are and how well you can work and how nice you can tend your corn and things in order on the place and how nice you will have things when i get home if so lucky

Page 4

Now boys i am in the woods about a quarter of mile from camp by my self sitting on a big writing these few lines to you Now i cicearly hope these few lines may find you boat well and in good heart about your work and not quarling about whitch does the most I would like to see you first rate and your young stock of all kinds colt calf lamb and pigs I exspect they will be nice when i come home so no more at present but remain your father write soon Henry Brown
