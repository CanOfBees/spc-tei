Camp Near the Mobeal Rail road June 6th 62 Caroline M. Brown Dear companion I now seat my self by a tree to write you a few lines to let you know how i am i feel verry well at this time but my feet And ancles swells verry much by times from some cause And i know not what they do not pain me verry much I hope these few lines will find you and the boys well I was down to the river and I saw 3 acres of land coverd over with hay oats and corn And more provision than I ever thought could be ruised in the United States we still have plenty of such as i have

Told you before our regment is about twelve miles below whare our camp is working on the road we have no idea whare we will go from here some say we will go to virginia and some say to mo and some to ky But we know not whare the rebels left corinth they ware well fortifide there and a verry large force they have scaterd scalurde and gone everry way from what they our men cant find many of them any more down here only small squads together and they wont stand to fight us we have memphis in

our possession again and the rail road leading from memphis to Richmond virginia i saw the cars yesturday i look natural to see them runing i cant give any news it is all getting to be verry old with us soldiers you had better think it apear to be a verry slow thing i will just say that major Free and Sam lions left here this morning for home lions is verry sick again i would like to see old Perry County once more i tell i saw a man cutting wheat yesturday it look

Nice I saw Thomas Martin he look first rate I must tell you what John hemerey and i done this morning we went out about a half a mile I took my gun out with me and i saw a nice little heifer one year old i supose and i drawed up my gun and nocked her down and took her in out of the damp we are a going to live well while it lasts we intend to live now we are getting down whare we can get some beef and sheep and hogs and chickens no more Henry Brown
