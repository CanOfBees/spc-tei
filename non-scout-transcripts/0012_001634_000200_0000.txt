General Orders To Colonel Allison Col Edwin Allison Greenville

Page 2

Sept 6th 1813 Sir From recent orders Rec d from Gov Blount and from an apprehension that an attack is Meditated by the Creek Indians on some part of the frontier of illegible it became my duty to order that defensive measures be immediately redobled to secure and protect the frontier of my division you will therefore without delay put the first Brigade of militia in the best possible state of defence by notifying them of the Meditated attack by the Creek Indians and requiring them to be ready to act at a moments warning to repel any invasion that may be made or strialured on any part of the Brigade now under your Command & treat the invading enemy to our peace and in the event of an attack made or of your knowledge of the approach

of the enemy to the frontier you will instantly order a Competent force not only to repel them but to Chalisr and as far as in your power utterly destroy them & persue as far as prudener will seictalr you will without delay Communicate to me all the information you learn acquire Relation to the movements & disposition of the enemy to gether with such orders as you may from time to time Issue with an account of all necessary expenditures for supply & you will be careful not to molest any friendly Indians but treat them with hospitallity & friendship so long as there Conduct is proper I am Respectfully your obt sert John Cocke orders similor to the alarm Isd to Genl Taylor but finding he is absant I send them to you now Commanding JC
