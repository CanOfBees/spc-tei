Wilson L. Ramsey To Bill Sale Sundries D Burford

Page 2

Know all men by these presents that I Wilson L. Ramsey have this day bargained and sold unto David Burford all the cherry plank and scantling which I now have at Rome; also conevey to said Burford all the plank and scantling which I have at Samuel Capligners mill consisting of about 2800 feet of poplar weatherboarding and cealing - also all my cooking utintials, house hold furniture &c &c. And I further agree to woork up the cherry plank which said Burford bought of Mr Moses Lawson and if I should sell any of the furniture to take the note or notes payable to said Burford: It is however understood and agreed that on any paying to said Burford about ninety dollars that then said Burford is to relinquish to said Ramsey that above property. I also agree to sell to said Burford my Saddle, Bridle, and martingill for the above purpose also: and it is further understood and agreed that said Burford may take possesion of any part or the whole of said property at any time and dispose of the same as he may think best to secure said debt. Witness my hand and seal this 11 day of July 1829 WL Ramsey seal S_P_ Hughes Ro. Po. Allison
