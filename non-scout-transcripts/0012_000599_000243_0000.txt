Union! June 6th 1862 Caroline M. Brown Dear little family All to gether i long to see you all together around our old table and that is not all i wish to be at home in harvest But i fear it wont be so that i can this harvest any way if i am sparred to get home Alold i hope the men will not sufer the crop to get to wast at any ratt the boys must be good to help with the grain and cary water to the hands and not grumble

Page 2

you had better try to get some boddy to cut the meadow and make the hay and mow it Away you will not have anough to let it out on the halves you had better try to save it all your self if you can you must let me know how the wheat look by this time and whether the weavel is in it or not and how your oats and corn looks i do hope you will be blest with good health while i am gone at any rate and good crops with the health

Page 3

I am sorry to say that I only sent you twelve Dollars the last time we drawed our pay we was paid off on the 22 of last month And we sent our money to Lexington By Mr Ogle in Mr Bone name he will let you know when he gets it and then you can use it to the best advantage James bone wrote to his father about it states theh amount to each one mine was $12 Dollars i owe the same old the cooks And i keep some for my own use

Page 4

I wish we was all at home with our family once more no more at present only that this may find all of you in the best of health my health is pretty good write soon I think the time long since i got a letter from you But i shall bare with it so help me god good by to you and the boys i hope we will soon meet again so good by to all Henry Brown
