My Dear Children James Andrew and Thomas you all like to have some Papers with Pictures in now i have gone to some trouble to get you them Papers And now i want you to take the time to read them and try and mind some of what you read there is some nice pieces in them James Send your Cousin Rosa Dailey one if you choose I hope that you are good Boys all of you i have promised you all a nice present if you mind and obey your Mother keep out of Mischief dont be climbing the fences nor the grape vine rack you may get hurt yourselves and when you do it you learn Sharon to do it as well Now i must quit and trust that that you will try and be good Boys Your Father

Page 2

I think that we will be paid and muster out at the same time that is Just my own opinion and i hope it may be so this morning i get off duty at six o clock i went through the market for to prise things Potatoes 75 cents a Peck common ham 27 cents a pound Lard 3 lb for a Dol Molasses 25 cents 1 Pint common Sugar 25 I went and weighed a loaf of our Bread too day to see if it was the right weight or not it was full weight which was 18 oz what is allowed for a days ration Cap Wylie is going to Duan in town so he told me too day I have nothing else to write that i know of no more than i feel tired and i want to finish this to night it may be that i shall be on guard tomorrow again after i came back from town this morning i cleaned my gun it took me till dinner time we have to keep them so Bright for the are examined every day So i went and washed myself the Towell and Hankerchief and then I mailed some Papers i took them down to the Post office myself then i came Back and commenced to get the Supper ready for the cooks was away
