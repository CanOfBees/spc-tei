March 5 1865 Nashville Tennessee Dear Wife I now the oppotinity this morning in writing to you these few lines hopeing they will find you all will at this time as it leaves me and alfred pretty well considering bad colds I have had a bad cold on me for two weeks but I hope I shall feel better in a few Dayes it as rained three days and nights and we have to sleep in the cold barick every night without any fire and that gives all bad colds I had the diarea one Day and night an alfred had it some but i hope in a few dayes he will be better of it I seen Mr. curtis an i spoke to him for alfred to work in the mess house an i got him a place to a wait an table he is to have twelve shillings Per Day that is fortey five Dollars a month an that will be good pay for him I hope he will be a good boy he can make mony theare

der wife we went to campainin and stayed theare on week and then came back to nashville and some of the men are staying here yet untill they go to another place to work I hope I shall be well in my health I shall stay three months I ham going on monday or tuesday with another gan of men to chop woad we are going to culunbey that is on the chatennagey railroad two dollar Per Day and gather some of the men from home in by are going with me Spencer is gone home he went back on Saturday an I will tll you about thing when I come home Mr Spencer went to Binghamton on Saturday afternoon and Mr Sloghis me this morning that he had talagraft to Louisville for him to come back he sad he would fetch him back again but dear wife if he is at home Dont you tell no one about it I will tell you when I come home my wife I must tell you that I have such a cold that I think I never had before

my longhs are so sore I have had chouch on me for more then a week now that I hope in a few Dayes I shall fell better we came from camp Irwin last wedensday the first march and I ham staying in baracks yet untill we go we have all we can Eate it dont cost anything we an government rating alfred is had the Direa a few dayse I spoke to Mr curtis an he said alfred should have some medderon for it dear wife I think if I ham well I shall only stay 3 months here I do not like tennessee at all with my kind love to you and my der henry an Austin you must kiss henry for me and Alfred sends his kind love to Austin and henry and mama and a kiss to you you all please to give my kind respects to mister and mrs King and I hope they are all well theare is two hundred to three hundred men now in the baracks now waiting to go to war now please tell austin alfred ad made out bill of rite he is leving with to uncle eoach

my dear wife I will send you by Express thiry dollars I went this afternoon and I sent you thirtey dollars by admes Express march 6 the moneey Erive in fore or five days I think you will get the money bfore if not plese to call at morgan epress and theare you will the packag money you must go yourself for the money nobody but you can draw the money as soon as you this letter you must write back to me directly I will send you the reicet I have I hve to show it if the the money is lost dear wife if you have wrote to me I neve had any letter send me word if you sent any letter campinin so if you did I will not it now you must decide to williaman not william have send me word send me all the news I am about Binghamton William Smith Nashville to now I colcude with my kind to your affectionate husband wilim smith write as soon as you can

Mrs Eliza SmithClinton St.BinghamtonN.Y.
