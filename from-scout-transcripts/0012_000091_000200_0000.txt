March 13th / 65 Tennessee My Dear Wife I sit me down again at Nashville to write I have written you at Indianapolis and Louisville I state this in order to know whether you have recieved these or not I described you the scenes which I wittnessed at Indianapo As for Louisville I cant tell you much about it I was taken so sick in the ears about two o clock in the morning I was warm enough sitting near the stove but i took a cramp in the stomach it must have been something like the cholera Morbus by what I was told others what had it I never felt anything like it in my life I could not straightened myself i felt as though there was a great pressure on my stomach I could hardly breathe I thought indeed that my time was up David of Old never Prayed more fervently than I did then I begged not to be

cut down in snu time of my life I took the half of the Paregoric at it did me good at all there was a man there what had good whisky he told me to take a mouthful of it I found relief shortly after Sabbath I felt stupid the best part of the day I ate no Breakfast but our Sargeant got me a cup of Coffee which i drank But i blame it to the drinking of coffee at Louisville it looked black and not with strength but with drugs i suppose it was some extract or other But Sabba dinner time i ate a little soup then we left at about 1 or 2 in the afternoon Sunday night i ate that sweet cake or a part of it i gave more than the half of it away that is the way that some has so much less to carry than others some wont provide bread nor water and here they will go around a begging all the time i will shut

Dear Anna we were marched out to day about 9. o clock to a new Barracks which is at the out skirts of Nashe there is several forts handy here what our men took from the Rebels Kentucky also is all cut up with Forts and Fortification most of Kentucky that we passed through was wild and swampy there was some country around Louisville though I would seldom see a nice farm house it was generally a log house some time a frame house I saw great many Niggers and Nigger-hutts Those fellows that was left behind is not come on yet they are the worst that belongs to the burnyr I dont care if they should never come here either there is some more of the same stamp have left Direct as follows David. J. Jones Nashville Tennessee Care of Cap Wiley. 78 Penna Vol
