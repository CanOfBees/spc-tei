Binghamton April 19 1865 my dear husband i now answer your kind and welcome letter that i received april 18 and i was dreadfull sory to here that your eyes are so bad i hope my dear husband you will get something for them and besure bath them every morning in cold soft water i hope your cold will by this time be better my dear husband i was so suprised when you said in your letter that you had not received any letter from me i have ritting three letters to you in answer to your letters that i received i have wrote each one the same day as i received yours i have not neglected an hour in ritting i am to an House to here from you not to neglect a chanche i began to feel very bad about you it was a month since i heard a word from you and i was about crazey and i can sugeg how you must feel about us i have sent ever letter where you sent the directions and i got Mr lect at the store to directe them for me i hope you will receive this one we are all well at this time thank god for so great a favour i shall be glad when the time comes for you to come home again

you must rite as often as you can it make me feel so good to here from you we have never been divided before my dear you wanted to know about things at home they are all right we are planting the garden we have got cleare through to the stump fence Mr desion let us have it we have planted peare and chabge seed and a small bed of onions and beets and forward poptatore there is none to be both my dear your trees look beatifull that largest pear tree is all notted full for flowering twig is got a blom on it the others all look good your roses all look good i have my flower garden fixed and some of my seed in boxes and they are all up Austin is devoted his time in the garden a great deal he is been very slack of work on account of the great flood i received the money quite safe and we have had to live on it a great deal my dear you are never out of my thoughts i wonder where you are and what you are doing Mr and Mrs Coley are very kind to me and susan and danile but my neibours are not so kind to me slater the butcher is moved in spencers house and i dont sosheate with thim and i will not and the neibors do so they dont like it they people says she is a dangerse woman i received a letter from alfred the same day that i received your last a month agoe he is not answered mine

Austin sends his kindest love to his father he say he longs to see you he will be glad when you come home again little henery send a thousand kises to his dear father he talks about you a great deal send me word my dear when you rite what wages you are getting i would love to be where you are and see as much as you have seeing it must be a great sight send me word how many soldyers there is in geniral thomas army it will be grand to go thro-ugh the state of old virgeney Virginia you will have a leell to tell us if you live and are well to come home Austin whants you if you see any little thing that is currious to take care of it for him my dear it was a most dreadfull shock to us all about our dear president seen shot by that fenishe devil just when he was wanted the most amongst us there never will come another that can fill his place he is abolished slavery how shocking it is to take away our best friend on earth and only procteror protector i hope and pray that villian will be brought to the worst of tortures on this earth can have but he is now an angle bright in heaven there is doubt about that direct your next letters to Austin smith john smith got my letter and opened it came in there lost Mr and Mrs king send there best respects to you they are all well rite as soon as you can pray doo rite Austin Smith So i must conclud with my kindest love to you my dear husband from your loving wife Eliza Smith binghamton New york the heat must be awfull with you and the thunder and lightnen about you think of me then
