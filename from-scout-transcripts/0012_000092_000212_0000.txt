Campe Near Decher Staction Tenn July 16th 1863 Brother Crit I receved your welcolm letter but had no chance to rite on till to day. we have had fine weather sence we have been here - but it rained most every day and night sence we left Murfreesboro I never saw as much rain as there has been this time of the year be four. our army is on a stand still and I doent see any sines of us moving on for Bragg has got so far a head the we will never

catch up with him a gain. there are derserters coming in by the hundred every day and say the Acoun tings is full of them a frade to come in. when we were fighting at Hovers Gap Bragg tolde his men when thay come on to our five and seven shoters for to lite out. so thay done it. thay just more than seadader I hearde that olde morgan has been up though Indiana and Ohio if he is that will wake the people up if any thing will. he knows better than to fool Round old Rosey now we have to many mounted Infentry of seven shoters
