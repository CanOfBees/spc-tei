Knoxvill Tenn March 30 164 Dear Father I sit down this morning to answer your letter which came to hand yesterday and was glad to hear that you were all well I am well at present and so are all the rest of the boys there is very little sickness in this department at this time when we first came here there was about 8 deaths per day but now it only averages about 2 per day I was sorry to hear of Leiz Fetrair committing suicide but it dont make much difference it is only another Shamburg gone So Adam has a daughter I suppose he can write another letter to Simon so that George Wert could get to read it We were paid off on the 23rd of this month I drew $29.90. Just 2 months & 9 days I say we were to recieve bounty but the Captain & Paymaster could not agree on the amount to be given the paymaster wanted to give $40 to new recruits and $50 to veterans the captain wanted $50 for all of us I do not know whether he will succed in getting it or not I intended to send $25 to you

Tell Josh Wert that I Address as before wrote him 2 letters since I am here and never receved an answer rebel against the Governement and I think if Rumley township would do that the Union forces might as well quit fighting for Vallandig was Elected Govenor in the township and this township carried the state I want to know how the election goes this spring and who is elected but it will be copperheads of course if that Gold pen has a case to it and is a good pen you may send it and if not a good pen I dont want it I seen old Gen Schofield the other day he looks about like a lager beer suloon with his red nose and cheeks his hair is already tinged with gray although he is only 35 years old the force in this Department is getting smaller and smaller everyday their are regts going home on furlough but none have yet returned the 7th and 9th Ohio and 9th Michigan Calvalry have been sent to Lexington KY to remain Do you know the address of any of the Rumley boys in the 74th if so give it to me in your next letter if Gen Grant can now see the
