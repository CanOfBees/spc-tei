Chattanooga Tenns monday moring June 19 1865 my de wife you need not write to me any in my dear an lovieng wife with the helpe of the Blessed Lorde I have taken up my pen this morning in writing to you these few lines hopeing that they may finde you all well at this time of wich I thanke god it leaves me quite well at this time thank god for that great blessen my dear wife I know you must feel very bad at this time at the death of our dear childe alfred who I little thought would die in this awful hot and suney southern town wheare hundreds are ding every day and every weeke my dear wife theare is Desises of all kinds in this country a great deal of the complaintes are brought on the poor men by haveing to drink such bad water

I do not know what day I shall get on as soon as I gan get my money I shall leave Chaggng at for nashvill in the morning my alfred only livid two day and one night after I got to nasvhill on saturday night that was on the tinth day of june on saturday night that I there and he Died on monday night at the our of ten oclock at night my der wife the last thing that I gave him isin his dear life was an small peace ice he sucked that aminet or two and I see him getting worse and after a few minits hes breath was gone I called to the wardemaster and tolde him that my dear childe was gone he saide to me he had suffered a great deal pain I stayed in nasvhill 5 days and came to Chattanooga on friday night on Satur moring I got on the train and wente to resecca in the St of george 16 miles beyonde ballan and got my discharge from boss kelogg my warkin time is all gon in to the office and I ham waiting to now get my kay for my dear I want to come back god bless you my dear and loving wife came

so my dear wife you may expect me in a weeke or ten dayes when you see the train pass you can look tor me I will hold up hat when I pas by my Lisey and to Eate such foode as the the poor man haves to Eate here to live on harde tack and salt pork some the men that belonged to our gan are died some weekes ago with the wiperfence and many more of them are now in the hospitle with broken leggs and other complaintes my wife I cannot say any more about this subject of thing now my dear wife if I live and have my Ealth I can till you all aboute the south wheare I have been and what I have seen on my traveling through Eastirn tennessee my dear wife I coldnot being to tell you all that I know about the suffering of the poor men I did it would make you heart ake I know that my dear alfred was a sufer before he diead I shall not forget his moenes moans and cries to me as long as I live

Page 4

I will take good care of money I earned my poor der and loving childe it greived me to the very hearte to see him Die he tried all that he could to speake to me and he coulde not and that hurte my mindso that I felte very bad it made me feel very bad that my heart was so full that I cred over my dear child it was very harde for me to be there and know one to speke to that that I little thought that I should see him die in this hospitle in tennsse my wife I can tell you all about it when I come home when I get to nashvill I shall go and get is blankets and his cloths what belonges to him in the hospitle wheare my alfred theare from 25 to 30 is men they all had the cronic dsease the docter he did not know what to do with them the poor souls wheare very bad off with that complaint with the 1100 dallors in it amad I was so glad for that since alfred died did you send me any letter to nashville yet I sent to my pargralt I will be home as san I gan

Mr. Austin H. SmithBinghamton Clinton StBroome Co.N. York
