Swan-pond 26th of March 1799 Hond Parents I now lift my pen, to shew you by writing the various ways of dealing God has with his Creatures particularly to me and my Family - On the 4th day of February last past God was pleased to let us see our Fifth son - "The Lord maketh the barren to keep house, and to be a joyful Mother of Children - we from this time went on trying to rejoice in God as the giver of all our Merries untill Saturday the Ninth instant when it pleased God to lay his afflicting hand upon Our Dear first born son Billy Baine Alexander - he was seized with a violent case which shewed symptoms of a Bloody Flux which the next day proved to be so - we applied and had medical aid, but all to no purpose he continued to get worse; about the tenth day his Flux appeared to abate but he was all but choaked with worms and was attacked most excrutiating pain about the region of his navell, which continued with but short intervals untill Thursday the thirteenth day about 12 oclock half an hour after which time he resigned up his breath, and closed his eyes in death! And his active spirit I humbly trust took his seat at the throne of God - there to praise his name thro-out the boundless ages of Eternity - On Friday the Twenty Second instant Mr Garrick preached his Funeral Sermon to a very tender audience from Acts 16th Chap & 31st verse - at which time my Brother baptized our Fifth son, whom we called Billy Baine Alexander - for his Brother who

who was yet there lying a breathless corp before our eyes - My Brother with his most affectionate wife came here on the Wednesday before my sone died and tarryed with us untill Saturday, Sister had began to wean her daughter two or three days before she came here, left her daughter at home, and had plenty of milk to give our youngest son whilst she was with us, which was a singular kind providence in our circumstance - I have related to you now the most particular occurrences that have taken place with respect to us - When I take a review of all those circumstances - and call into view also the supports afforded to us in affliction, I must Let to my Seal and say "the Lord is good, I charge my soul to be silent and not suffer one murmuring that to arise - I discover the great goodness of God in having all thro his word such a rich variety of Consolatory Trusts for us to be supported with, and on which we may feed if God is pleased with them to give us the light of his Holy Spirit to apply them - which he in his own good time is sure to do - " Dea may the hands of life unloose " dissolve my love " Millions of infant souls compose " The family above " Their feeble frames my power shall raise " And mould with heavenly skill: " I'll give them tounges to sing my praise " And hands to do my will - " His words the happy Parents hear " And shout with Joys divine " Dear Saviour, all we have and are " Shall be fore ever thine Rippon Hymn 556
