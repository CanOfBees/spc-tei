Nashville Tenn April 4th/65 My Dear Wife Tuesday Morning after what we call Guard Mount I take the Peasure of Sitting down in my bunk to write you a few lines in hope that you and the children Also Robert and his family are all well as it leaves me at present through Mercy I never felt better than I do now notwithstanding the hard duty we have to perform saying nothing about the hard living and the hard sleeping hard bed and hard Pillows But that is nothing after a man gets use to it I had intended writing to you yesterday but all the men was on duty then I had to carry water for cooking and in the afternon Cap Wylie sent after me to help him move his quarters and fire up after getting through with him i intended writing but the News of taking Richd and Petersburg created such

an Ethusiasm in the crowd that between the firing the guns at Ft Negley and the cheering of the men it was imposible to do anything This morning Grubbs is not very well i got up at the break of day and made some tea for him and I the first cup of tea that i drank since i left you it was good. This morning i am on duty at the Head Quarters the most Particular place of any so many Officers to salute I thought I would have a great deal of news to give you to day I had intended to go to Ft Negley I also intended to go to the Capitol but I had no time as yet but some day i will go and see them When I was at Capt yesterday Cap Boggs got a letter from Allegheny City Stateing that Lawyer Marshall were collecting the Bounty and that he had collected all that had required of him I thought there was some scheme

on hand I know that Marshall dont collect it for nothing I wish you had mine It may be that Robert could get it for you because he has a great deal of influence and if you will get it pay off all we owe except what proposition you was going to make Put it all in Wills hands. I thought we would have had it long before this I should like you would give me David Quail s address I want to write to him I dont know when our James is gone to Sarah's yet if not you might get him to write a few lines and Andrew too I asked for Sarah s Address to and your Father but you are kept so busy you cant think of every thing you dont know how glad I would be to see Sharon and the Babe But I hop to see you all yet if the Lord Will I should like to know whether James and Andrew has

Page 4

been to see you yet I have nothing Particular to write you at this time I am in a hurry to get this Mailer M- leaves at 12 noon give my respects to Robert Wife & children I wish they would with you till I come back give my respects to your Father I should have wrote him but there is so may of the same name on that street he may never get it If you will see Old Mr Varnhorn give him my respects Also Mr & Mrs White next Door Tell Mr White that there is a great deal of difference between Soldiering and Carpentering Although I am not in my views of it I suppose that Mrs Cornelius is your next door neighbor by this time that old gentleman you spoke of let me know if he is yet or not Now Anna Kiss all the Children for me and recieve the same from me Although absent in body but ever present in mind. Yours David
