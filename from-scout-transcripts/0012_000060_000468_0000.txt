Madison, July 16th, 1880. My Dear Dr. Ramsey -- I wrote you yesterday -- & last evening I recd. your favor of the 12th. One thing I neglected yesterday to ask about -- page 230 of yr. History you say Campbell, Shelby, passed along the valley of Gap Creek: Is this stream one of the head waters of Watauga -- or is it a water course heading on the Eastern declivity of Yellow Mountain? You speak of Bright's Trace -- no doubt truly; but do "Bright's Place" -- where? I sometimes think it was a clearer spot of perhaps 100 acres -- on the very top of Yellow Mtn., where Gene. D. Campbell

Page 3

of Abingdon told me the men paraded -- but gave no specific name to any locality. But Bright's may have been beyond. I hardly expect to settle these matters -- I could have done it 20 years ago; but those who knew are gone. Mr. Sildin (or Filden?) Ridge I have not heard of before. In haste -- Truly Yours, Lyman C. Draper. Very Truly Yours, Lyman C. Draper.
