Allegheny City July 24th/65 My Dear Husband (I allmost forgot the dear thist time dident I) well I received two letters this morning and Tom & Andie a paper apiece I sent you another letter last week with two dollars more in it I am so glad that you are better I was so uneasy about you I was afraid that you had something of the disease that the soldiers are subject to our; children are remarkably healthy and I am so thankful to the great Giver for it I am carefull too about what they eat and to keep them warm enough when it is damp chilly weather Martin Graham and his father have been very sick Mr Grahams was derangement of the liver and kidneys with cholera morbus Martins chills and fever Mr Mr Cleard is still living but not much better he has now camp fever Mr Anderson is a good deal better his was derangement of liver I was very poorly
