Allegheny City June 20th 1865 My Dear Husband I thought surely I would get a letter from you today but I was doomed to disapointment for I got none but a paper for Tom and one for Andie I got one from Jimie this morning his aunt says that he is right well contented and well he dont say home is his letter at all he said that they got one from you last week I have not got any boarders I do wish you were home I would not I suppose think so much about it if the war was not over and so many getting home I think they might send you too but I supose you will tell me that I am like Grubbs wife about coming home but indeed it is natural for one to do so Tom set Mrs Whites mouse trap in the cellar and he caught 9 nmice since morning dong you think that is doing pretty well, well I am glad that they got them Mr Cornelious got home yesterday Bob is going to have supper for one of the Anderson girls that got married they sent me an invitation Sharon says ofthen Oh when me Pa mon home

but they would have to send me some money first to get some clothes for the children before I could go and I know they wont do that so I will have the pleasure of staying at home I could not enjoy myself when you are away at any rate so I wont try to go, it is raining here now I put up some strawberries I preserved them I got the sugar from reeds they took a pair of stools so did Will Gekley but I did not lift money either placebut it is all the same I want to send this down to the post office before night indeed david I am sorry you are not in favor of me getting a sewing mashine for we need one badly and it would soon pay for itself I do hope you fellows will get next or dered out I saw archi campble was so drunk that he could hardly walk dont tell John so but he has been drunk all day write soon yours affectionally anna M Jones
