Binghamton May 30 1865 my dear husband it is with pleasure i answer your kind and welcome letter that i have just received from you May 30 and i was so glad to here you say you wa so much better as it leaves us al well bless god for so great a blessing i have been very poorely but bless god i am better i have ha the ereiples broke out in my face but it is better now we are very pleased to here you say you was coming home i hope you will come safe my dear i long to see you i have not heard from Alfred since i rote your last letter and then he was wes and he said he was coming home the 18 of june and i hope he will come with you i rote to him

about his money i told him as i do you to send it by Adams express office company it would be the sopety way Mr Mathers caled the other day to inqure after your health and he wished me to give his best respects to you and tell you to send your money to me by Adams express he says he is read of several bien robered of all they had worked had for but you can please your self rite me a nother letter when you are coming if you can when you know Austin is well he send his kind love to you and little henery is well he send you a thousand kises to you he goes to scholl

i have not much to rite this time i will tell you the more when you come home there is nothing fresh in binghamton only houses going up all round us i hope you will not stop in Nashvill it is a dreadfull place for roberers i hope you will look out and dont sit down and goo to sleep there if you do you will be roberded be sure keep your eyes open and take care of Alfred i send you a thousand kises i long to see you i can hardley wait for you both to come so i must conclud with my kindest love from your affection wife Eliza Smith
