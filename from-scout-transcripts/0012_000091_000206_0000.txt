Nash Apl 23 My Dear Wife This Saturday Evening I take the pleasure of writing you a few lines in hope that they may reach you and find you all in good health as it leaves me at present through Mercy i think i shall not finish this letter this evening for we have to go out on Parade this evening at 5 o clock I came of duty this murning at 9 o clock last night was right cold my last watch was in the morning from four till six last night when i went too lay down i could find no Pillow so i took a lime stone and throued the cape of my Over coat over my head and slept sound till i was roused up I do not mind of dreaming though like Jacob when he had a stone for a pillow I saw Willm Zeager yesterday i was on guard near to where he was so i thought that i would call and see

him it was lucky that i went too because if i would want to see him at any future time i would have some trouble to find him I had heard that they were breaking up some of the Hospitals and as i was going to dinner i passed one hospital that was all vacated so i started round to him so all the Patients were being moved out of the hospital he told me that there was some sent home to their own states and some to their Regiments and those that were not able to be sent home were sent to other Hospitals so he was going to another Hospital himself that evening he would rather to go his Regt though he says Although he has but four months to serve While I think of it i alwas forget to name it Charley Magee is sick every once in a while he is sick now he goes though the boys calls him scarce of fat others calls him a needle i overheard him talking one evening saying that his Father had seven hundred Dollars which he had earnt and that his Father was keeping a store in Pittsburgh Also he was blowing that he had spent 150 Dollars since he has come out to the Army I had a notion to say something but i did not

Dear Anna i mind of you mentioning in your Letters several times about feeling so lonesome I know you do and I know that i would be so glad to have your Company as you would be to have mine and i hope that our wishes will be granted yet Sabbath morning this is nice morning but cold last friday i saw some fine Magnolia trees here they were from fifteen to twenty ft high they are not in Bloom yet I also saw some grapes that were only just formed the first that I have seen this year I have not seen Mr Camel since last Wednesday John has not seen him either John thinks that he is gone to Chatanooga John is well he wrote home yesterday himself I have no Particular news to give you it is reported here that Johnson has surrendered but it is not official though I received a Supplement to the dispatch from Mr Wills on last friday we get the news here before you do there. I have had no letters from you since that which brought me the tidings of your Father s Death and the one from James our sone at the same time I shall give you the dates of what letters that i sent you so that you will know if you have received all or not I shall write a few lines to James & Andrew & Thomas

My Dear Children I write you these few lines in hope that you are all well As i am through mercy I think a great deal about you when you are fast asleep at night and wehn you are in your bed I have to be down town watching houses for fear of the Rebels setting them on fire And another thing I should like to know whether you are good Boys or not Do what your Mother tells you do all you can to help her Also watch Sharon and the Babe keep them off the street The Babe if she lives will be running around Always read at night and learn your Lessons well I hope you will do this for your Father David
