State of Tennessee County of Greene To S.P. Carter Provost Marshal Genneral or the proper military Commandant Knoxville Tennessee The undersigned union citizens of said County, do most respectfully bring before you the case of John smith a paroled prisoner taken at the Surrender of Vicksburg. In a few days after the surrender Mr. Smith came home and remained there until after the falling back of Gen Wilcoxs Brigade, working on his fathers farm, but in consequence of being told that if he remained until after the occupying of this portion of the state by the Federal Army, That he would be arrested and held as a prisoner of war, he went to Smith Co, Va. as we have been informed, and worked as a private citizen on a farm until a short time ago he came back to his fathers where he was by a portion of Col. Footes command arrested, and as we understand, taken to Knoxville These as we understand then are the facts of the case. We would further state that said Smith is a truthful, moral, well behaved young man, and several of us have heard him

Page 2

say that he never intended to fire another gun until he was regularly exchanged and to our knowledge has never arried arms, nor misbehaved in any way We respectfully as you to examine his case, and see if he can be released upon bond, or in some other way consistent with the rules and articles of war All of which is respectfully submitted. Greeneville June 24th 1864 Names Samuel McGaughey G. Jones William S. McGaughey John McGaughey Azor Koontz Solomon good Isaac C. Dobson Samuel Brittan David M. Dobson Thomas S. Dobson Sam'l S. Cook
