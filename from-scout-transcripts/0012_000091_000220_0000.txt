Nashville June 3 65 My Dear Wife This is Saturday and near dinner time I have been busy all Morning i was up at 1/2 Past four I am up between four and five every Morning After getting up i went about a quarter of a mile to a spring house to wash and was back bfore roll call which is at 5 o clock some of the fellows will not get out of bed till Breakfast which is six o clock then they Jump out of bed and eat without either washing either their hands or face Then after Breakfast went to the river to wash some clothes that is about 2 miles from camp there is no soft water any nearer than the River but they will have the Hydrants in the Barracks in two weeks but then they will move us some where else at least Just as soon as we are comfortably fixed they move us At the Last Place

we were at we had everything so convenient there i had made little stools and a Place to write on and a wash board we had to leave all behind for we could not carry them at the time and to go back the next day would be useless I recieved 2 letters from you one on the first and the other on the second they were dated the 28 & 29 I was glad to learn that you and the children were all well as i am through Mercy I guess Mr Camel is home by this time he can tell you how i Look and if i was when he was here I have not been on duty since last Monday i am Jubbin's at the Barracks so i have had no time to go around here so as to give you an account of the country around here it stands of the Property of Rebel Genl Cheatham and his three Sons lived all in sight of each other they have a Splendid house apiece and owned 18 hundred acres of Splendid Land here all in one Batch they tell me that there was a Large Orchard here but our fellows cut them

Down at Least there are great great many Stumps around there any way The Land is delightful it lays There is also a splendid Spring near one of the houses it is arched over like an oven then about an hundred yards there is a splendid Brick Spring house two story high it is floored with flags and about 2 feet all round the floor is about a foot lower where the water is So the Ballance of the floor is dry there is four or five Stone Steps to go down it is in an awful bad order though this is excellent water there i think at least i think it the best that i have drank since i left home indeed i think it Better than the Allegheny River it is a good deal clearner at any rate These Big People can enjoy themselves first rate in this country for they have their houses are well shaved so they can Darken it up and take at any time There is some splendid county seats around here but i think they are pretty much Catholics for they have little Crosses hanging in the Janzens There is two catholic

Right in sight of each other I believe there is no United Presbyterian Church here There are a freat many soldiers within a few hundred senes of each other here there is no less than Seven Regiments Right in sight of us I am glad that Tom is a good Boy i hope that Andrew will be better i would make him do what i wanted him to do you said that i was silent on the subject of coming home i hate to write about it when i cant tell anything about it you cant rely on anything you read in the paper There is good many rumors out but they have no foundation if we come home in a couple of months i shall be satisfied Grubs Wife is writing all the time about that i am sick of writing too her she wants him to get his discharge I heard all that kind of eatables was very cheap in Allegheny at present No more at Present I remain your Husband as Faithful as ever It is just one o clock afternoon David
