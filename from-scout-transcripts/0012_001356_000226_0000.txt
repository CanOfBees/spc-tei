Chattanooga Tennsee Sunday morning may 21 1865 So my dear wife I recieved your kind and wellcome letter on Saturday moring the twentith day of may and I was so glad to here that ou was all well when you last wrote to me I was sorry to here you say my dear little henry had been so sick but I by this time he is quite will again and austin I hope he is better of the bad cold that he is had now my dear and loveing wife I thank god your letter found me comfortable then and at time my cold is a good deal better than it as been for some time but still my longs are very weake now I ham sometimes very sort of breath by times but my dear wife I hope when I come come home I will rest my self awile and then I hope I shall feel better then I was sorry to here

I ham going rate if my dinner the death of Mr Dephew was dead but I was not at all suprised of that because he had been sick so long atime my dear Lisey you didn sende me worde what Mr Sebey it was that died in Binghamton wa it Mr Sebey on akelict street send me word when you write again to me my wife I ham now staying in Chattanooga at the mens house no 23 and 24 theare is 18 men here they are working in the Streets Poileing up lumber from the railroad wich from cleveand and knox and from Blewspringis my wife I been a good many miles on that roade we left MacDonald Station on the 11 day of may and mr meares and all the stree men are staying at mac doanales stion they getting timber out of the woods with oxan teames there

he said enough about me now I note want any thing about that old drunken Duchman my dear and loveing wife I write these few lines to tell you that we all expect to be discharded on the first of june they are all paing off all the worke men in chattanooga that they dont want some of our men was paide las weeke and went home on Saturday theare is only 100 and 50 men in this construction core left now we dont thinke we will be here any longer then two weekes more before we are discharde from worke all the men in the department are paide off and gone home but us and two more gans and we expect to be the next called in the first of June my dear wife I blessed Lord will give me my ralth and strengith to come home then and find you all well at that time with my kindest love to you my dear wife and to my dear austin and my dear Henry

three kises for henry and two for lisy and austin I have not been here here only by your letters but I hope he wil at this time thank god I dont have to worke so harde no I care the water for the cookhouse and cut the woode and I have a good time now our house is in front of lookeout mountain Lookout Mountain it looks bautfull when we get up in a morning and open back door and see the solar camps and tents and the mucse playing at 5 oclok my dear wife you must write back to me soon as you have this letter then I would have time get another from you by the time we leave here my dear I must conclude with my kinde love to all my kind respects to mr and ms coley and Ringe and goerge hau William Smith Chattanooga in care SC Mearse Chattanooga Tenn I will send you all the erticlers perticlers in the next letter my de Lisy a sweet kiss to you
