James Robertson which was one of the oldest Rules of the part age nor must I forget in connection of this hand Saw, to say it give me a Senciar pleasure to see the vauel pf the Butte are of Wm. Cribbs which I Regret to Say Died in Dyer County 5 or 6 weeks since and had I not Acknowledged in geting these ameant relics at the time I did they never would have never found there way to the Historical Society of Tennessee I have faded in securing Col David Crocketts Journals hunting gun (Old Betsy) I Rode 78 miles to get so valuable a Rifle But faded I went to his old neighborhood and was there Informed that Col Crockett took old Betsy with

a very good Mill which was built by Major Rutherford in the 1821 or 22 this was the first Mill built on that portion of the Chicksaw Parks from the Spring to the mouth of Branchwea about 200 years and while Major rutherford was Eseaming the Countery South of the Bluff in 1784 with a view of making his location for a company of men who were the owners of a larg amount Land Warents which had been Issued to the officers and soldiers of the Revolutionary War with great Breten in 1776. One of the men belonging to Major Rutherfords company killed a very larg Buck near the mouth of Said Spring hand and from that day to the Present the River O hanon has been known as the Forkhiden River and its various Tributarys which on a map represents a Deers Horns justly entitles it to the name Forked Deer At the mouth of or near this Spring Stands a large Secamore Tree which Major Rutherford marked as his Beginning Corner of the Forked Deer Connection which continued Several hundred Thousand Alln I have seen this old Secamore 100 Times have
