Camp Near Murfreesborough Tenn Feb 23rd 1863 Brother Crittenden I thought a long as this is pay day I woulde rite you a few lines to let you know that we are all well at presant. I receved a letter from you to day and here that you have got the measels. you must minde now and not run out in the Colde for if you cetch colde on them it will go pritty harde with you. I have fifty eight Dollars now and will live something like home now. I was out Foragen for tow days and we had a good time we have a good suply of fresh pork

Page 2

and Chickens honey turnips apples and sutch things as we need. we are a foragen or on picket most every day. but we have got annew Col from the 17th Ohio and I think he is a first strate Col from the Appearnces of him. he went up to Gen Negley and seen him a bout putting us on duty so mutch. Negley promis him here after that the duty woulde be mutch lighter. I wount you to rite and tell me what Brigade Henry Richard is driving team for or when you rite to him tell him to rite to me for I havent had a letter from him sence he left from our house. tell Father that

T. F. Anthony sent him a Check of $50 Dollars. well I must stop riting for I havent mutch to tell you we are a doing well and very well at that for the weather is a getting warm the cars runs clear thought now and have plent here to eat and wear Southgate Cregmile 2 Division 2 Brigade and the olde 69th rite often for we doent get many letters now. I doent think thay come thought.
