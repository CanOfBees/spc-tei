Dr. J.G.M. Ramsey Knoxville, Tennessee.

Page 2

In acknowledging yr. favor after 2d last evening, I was necessarily hurried in order to get off my return missile by the earliest possible conveyance. My Mecklenburg work has hung fine so long, I am almost getting vexed with the delay -- & am resolved in a few days to send on the ms. any way. I am not yet certain of a publisher --- a firm / Claxton / Remsen & Co-- Phila-- will examine it, & then determine. I presume gre this reaches you, the request has gone to May Nelson for King letter-- I hope so, for a single day gained at this late period will be a

Page 3

help to me -- a great help. I hope you will not fail to have it sent me. Not in any special hurry aft. Mr. Loudon. Another insuing on that point, wh. you can respond to at your leisure. That is, whether you happen to know any intelligent oldsettler in the region of Tellico Plains (where there is a P.C. ) or old Fort Loudon -- or at Morgan Town nearly opposite? -- I find it convenient to write the name Loudon, when the old Lord in whose honor it was named, wrote his sign--manual Loudoun. I send you a copy of my associate's work on Crawford-- so you may judge, with his adeptness at composition, & good judgment in selecting & combining materials, with my wealth of historical collections & my long and patient sifting of matters-- what will he likely to be the outcome of our joint efforts. While I send you the volume yet I hop when you read it, that you wd. write him B. a letter expressing of whatever pleasure its perusal may have afforded you. I know it wd. gratify him as coming from one of the veteran historians of the West . Truly Yours -- Lyman C. Draper.
