June 24th 1863 Morristown Tenn. Friends at home I now take the oppurtunity of writing you a few lines and they will tell you that I am well and I hope you all are well I have got to this place and is very tired and has hered a heap of news mor than I can tell you but any how the yankees has made a nother rade in Tenn and has done a good deel of damage and is gon back they commenced at Knoxville and come up in 15 miles of this place and burnt 2 bridges and a great deel of other damage I cant tell you half what I have hered I have not time and I would not wrote yet but we cant here where our Redgment is and if they are gon from the gap I new it would be a long time before you could here from me

but it may be that I can give you more news when I write again I Shall write but few lines this time it will take us 3 days more to get to the gap we have had a good spell of weather for traveling and prettey good faire . I left home with 12 dollars and got here with 6 and spint 2 dollars for tobaco and 1 for a dram we will start on to the gap in a few minuts and I will not write much more and you write as soon as you get this and if we are not at the gap the lettes will follow after me we have got meat and bread to do us to the gap so I will git for this time and try to give you full history of the yankee rade when I write again. Nothing more this time W M Cloer Direct to Mr W M Cloer Cumberland Gap Taswell County Tenn 62 NC Troops Co D in care of Capt R M Henry
