The Subscriber wishes to know on what terms the Insurance Company of North America will underwrite the herein after described House situated in the Town of Reading Berks County for seven years on due proof being made of the truth of the description A two Story dwelling House situated in the Centre Square near the Court House_ the outside and inside walls of Brick the outside walls fourteen Inches thick_ The House is twenty one feet in Front and about fifty four feet in depth erchinie of a small Room and a Kitchen with Rooms over them adjoining the back of the House and built also of Brick_ There are in the House three large well finished Rooms down Stairs with Fire places in them, also a small back Room without a Fire place in which there is a Iron Stove the pipe of which goes into the Kitchen Chimeny_ The Kitchen is paved with Brick. The Rooms up stairs comes pond with those below and are well finished. the Garris is ceiled and the Roof is Cedar, there is a trap Door on the front building just by the Stack of Chimnys from which you can throw Water down or cover the top of the Chimnys- there is also a door to go on the back buildings and a ladder to go to the top of the back Chimnys_ The Kitchen Ashes is conveyed by a hole in the hearth to the ash hole which is a large vault in the Cellar cvoerd and closed all round with a Stone &Brick wall except one side which is walled three feet high _ the ashes from the other Fire places is conveyed to the same place _ there are no trees near the House which fronts North on Penn Street 150 feet Wide; to the East is joyne a Brick House the property of Mr__Thomas Dirndas now in the tenure of W. Lewis Janoby who keeps a dry good Store _ which house is bouded to the East by a vacant Lot of Sixty feet being the Corner of Calhon hill _ Street which is Eighty feet wide _ on the Fourth there is a vacancy

of about sixty feet from the Kitchen to a Brick Stable and Frame Carriage House on a twelve foot Alley _ to the front there is a vacant space of twenty one feet bounded by a Frame dwelling House filled in with Brick and Rough Carr and a stone back building _ There are three Fire Companies in Reading well supplied with Buckets to and there are two Fire Engines kept in Brick Houses within sixty feet of the Premises, and within two or three hundred feet ladders hooks to are kept to be rnd in case of Fire _ There is a large Cistern (with a Pump in it) in the yard supplied with Rain Water from the Roof_ there is one Pump within thirty feet of the Door and twelve other Pumps within convenient distance all in good order and well supplied with water_ the House is owned &occupied by an Attorney at Law is in perfect good order all but the Walls being nearly new, and the House exclusive of the Ground is worth upward of 2000 Dollars _ on this House it is proposed to Insure 1600 Dollars _ A two Story Brick House adjoyning the above the property of W. A two Story dwelling House situate in Callon hill Street in the town of Reading built of Brick with brick Kitchen about 25 feet front and thirty deep exclusive of the back buildings which are also of Brick and two Story ___ Two Rooms down Stairs beside a Passage between the back Room &Kitchen __ Four Chambers _ Fire places in all the Rooms except one of the Chambers and the Garrets - Trap Door and brick arch holes __ Bounded on the West by Callon hill Street which is Eighty feet Wide _ on the North there is a Vacant Space of about 35 feet which is bounded by a Brick House, on the East by Vacant space of about two hundred feet, on the South there is a Vacancy of sixty feet bounded by a Brick dwelling House _ there is one Pump within forty feet of the Door and three others within a convenient distance all in good order and well supplied with Water and there is a Pond of Water within about three hundred feet of the House __ The House is well finished in good repair and worth exclusive of the Ground 1400 Dollars ___ On this House it is proposed to insure 1000 Dollars _ Reading Mr. W. 1800 Marks Jno.
