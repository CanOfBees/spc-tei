Binghampton May 22 my dear husband i now rite these few lines to you in answer to your kind and welcome letter that i received May 22 and i was glad to here that you was some better and i hope by this time you are well as it leaves us all well but myself i am not felling very well to Night i am worring about you i hope my dear you will get your discharge the first of June and come home as soon as can get home i need you home as much as the south needs you and more two i am got to be awfull nervese since you went away that if you stay away much longer i cannot stand it we have such thunder and lightning and such heavey rains about every day or two it was with ever thing
