Chattanooga Tennassee May 30 1856 1865 tuesday morning donte woree ourself you can help nonler then my dear and loveing wife I received your kind and welcome letter on the 29 and and I was glad to heare that you was all quite well at that time when you wrote to me my wife I thank god your letter found me pretty well at the present time bless god for that all though my longes are very weeke now I ham very short of breath at diffrentimes some nights when I lay myself down to sleepe I have the bathing at my heart so I cannot go to sleep for two or three ours and nights not hardley any sleepe at all and have to worke all the day while right roloc at night so my dear lisey you may think I have to be on the frit enough all the day longe my dear wife I felt sorry to here you say my dear lisey

you have time write me and then when I write again tell austin I bouth him a solder overcoate my dear wife I feel very bad because I ham disapinted in comeing home I thought I would be home by the middle of June so I dont know when I shall be home I dont expect that I will be discharged before next august but my dear wife you must not fret your self so much as I ham wall I hope I shall be well as long as I stay heare in this suny and hot south mr mears come from mac Doanald Station to Chattanooga last night and he tolde us that we weare all got to go to Atlanta in gorge and we dont know how longe we will be gone theare some say a month and some two months the train is to come on thursday morning and take us out on the road for george we are going to stope at Dalten Dalton first and Kingston to six some week first but will

so my dear wife peapes the next letter of may win george I with we may not go theare at all it a hot climat climat theare now to go so my dear wife you must keepe your spirits up as well as can and dont worke to hard in the garde nomdee then you can helpe with my kindest love to you and a sweet kiss from me and three kisses for my dear little Henry and austin I dont know any thing about alfred but I he is well he was very in kinde to me I wrote two letters to him and he never answered one of them I hope will take care of is money my dear wife I wish you would aske austin if he would lende me two dollars and lende it in the next letter if you could speare it it is harde to aske you for the same when I ham got so much may owning and and cannot get it to you sende a two dollars if you can put it in a small wate bill in the letter I will pay it to you again when I come home it is harde to be here and not onc sent of money I couldnot percure here with my kind love to you my dear and loveing wife yours William Smith blew
