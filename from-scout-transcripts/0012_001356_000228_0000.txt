Chattanooga Tennsee Tenessee tuesday aftenoon June 6 1865 my dear and loveing wife I write these few lines in answer to your kind and wellcome letter that I received from you on tuesday morning the 6 day of June and I thank god it found me pretty well at the present thank the Blessed Lorde for that since I have been in Chatt Chattanooga me Ealth had been some better these last three weekes then it had before but still my longes are weeke my dear wife I donte expect to get stronge again untill I come home again to binghamton and then if I live and the Blessed lorde gives me my Ealth and strenth to come again my dear wife I hope I will have something good to eate I have lived longe enough on hardtack and salt pork in the hot ang suney south Lisey I ham gown

night six oclock for mens suppr it is amost my dear lisey I ham grone very poore this last two or three weeks but my dear wife the sun is so hot here that it almost melts one dawn the weather is been very hot here the last two weeks some times I cannot hardly breathe it is hot by dea wife I dont know what kinde a weather you have out north I some times with you had some of it out north with you said in your last letter that it had been very rainey at Binghamton and I by this time you have better weather my dear and loving wife I feel very lousy that when I wrote to you in the last letter but one that I should be home in the middle of June as well all expected to have our Discarge then but one gan of men wheare ordered down on the atlanta in the stat of george Georgia and we are

good by and god bless you all I must go and sit my table my dear lisey I ham going away away from Chattanooga tomorrow morning down to atlanga and we dont think that we will be backe intill the last day of June or the first of July and I hope then if I ham alive and well and come back to Chattanooga I shall then try and get my discharge and then come home and see you all we are bein tolde by men in chattanooga that when we come back from atlanta that we will all get our discharge then my dear wave I was sorry to hear you say that you had the eicpes in your face again but I hope you are beter when you get this letter to reade Lisey you would like to be here now the black berries are ripe here I had some last night for super and they were good and I wished you had been there with me and had some of them

my dear wife you must not fret yourself to much I hope that I shall be some as soon as I can for I long for the time to come to see you as much as you do to see me my dear I ham going away on the train tomorrow morning down in george to Kingston that is 40 miles from chattan my dear wife I sent worde to you in my last letter that I wrote to you to aske my dear austin if he would lende me two dollars of money to by me some few eggs to eate and milk I cannot eat salt porke and hard tack dear wife you can send it in the next letter that you write to me my dear and loving wife I have not much more to say now I must conclude with my kindest love to you my dear wife and a kiss from me and to you with my love austin and my pretty boy henry olde mearse went home and left us all behind he came away from here and left us write to me about my binghamton as soon as you can and send me send me all perticlers
