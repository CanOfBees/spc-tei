Sunday April the 2 1865 Bulls gap Tennsee Bulls Gap, Tennessee my dear wife I now take the opportunity in writing to you these few lines hopeing to find you all well at the time at wich it leaves me not very well at this time I have not got well of my cold yet my eyes are very Bad the sun is so ot here and I had a strong inflmation in them but I ham going to ask the Docter to give me some watrye water to dress them with it as been such heavey rains here the thunder and lighting heare is awfull some times it rais two or three days and nights and after that the sun comes so ot that it burns thru your cloths and that makes it so miserables heare

my dear wife I think it very straing that you Dont write to me if you have writen I have had not one letter from you yet the last letter I wrote to you was on the twenty teuleveth of march dear wife I must tell you I pray to the blessed lord for my Ealth and strength as Long as I have to stay heare I must tell you that we have to put up with a good deal heare in this construction coare there is three hundreed men in this coare we all belong to general Shermas Department if you weare see worke here you would never for get the sight to see the bridges burned and Railroad tore up the Bridge we are bulding

now is beteem eaght amd nine hundred feet long and the next one from 12 to 15 hundred feet long this railroad goes to richmont Richmond and we are following up the road all the way into vergeney Virginia genearall Stamers that are to repar the road cleare to hopers fery Harper's Ferry in vergeney Virginia and I hope we shall go through theare and then I hope we shall get strong and come home this place is bulls gap seventy miles from Bristil and when we get to Bristil we then will be on the line of vergeney Virginia there we are now in Eastern tennesee three hundred and 50 miles from Nashville dear wife I ham a good many hundred miles from you now but never the wase

nere the worse that I shall long for the time to see you again with my kind love you and a kiss for you and my dear henry and austin and tell them I as well as can be expected at this time I have not heard anything of alfred since I left nashvill that on was on the 11 march I will write to him soon as I can he told me he would write to you when I left nashvill please to send all the partickler about home and give my respects to Mr King and and familey and tell them pretty well but have a cold send me workd weather alfred wrote to you my dear wife write to me as soon as you can there peach trees are in full Bloo they look hansom my kiss William Smith I conclude love to you all
