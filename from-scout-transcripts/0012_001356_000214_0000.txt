Binghamton May 6 1865 Saturday my dear husband it is with pleasure i rite these few lines to you in answer to your kind and welcome letter that i received May 4th and i was glad to heare that you was well and your eyes so comfortable as it leaves us all well thank heaven for so great a blessing i worried about your poor eyes i was afraid you would be blind but i am so thankfull to god that he is restored to you your eyes well again and i hope he will restore to you your health whilst away on forineng foreign soil while away from freinds and those who loves you you are all to me in this world and my dear children i hope we shall be the world to each other darling great sorrow has come to us all but out of sorrow strenght is born in each other love we will gather strenght and chearfull receive the sunlight as it Homes in upon our hearths and homes we had a dreadfull shock lossing our dear president and cheif Magstrate by a most villiansos murderer but they have shot him and they are a going to bring the other of his assocatous to justises but the lord will over take the murders for vengence is mine and i will repay it say the lord the lord will punish them at the last day for he says in his word no murder shall enter the kingdom of heaven and one that will rise up and murder a second moses when he so bravily like moses was on the verge of entering in the promis land but let us hope but there is nothing in this world to count of or hope for but still let us like the dromed man catch at a straw let us hope that our president Andrew Jonston Andrew Johnson the succeder of our late president

may be like Josuh when he led the children of israel in the promis land moses was not suffered by god to enter in the promis land when he was on the verge of entering god smote the rocks that moses should not enter and Josuh led them through and god ordainded it to be that Abraim lincoln Abraham Lincoln was not to do no more on earth he had gained libertey and his work was finished god ordained a better purpose for him his mission is ended on earth he is laided quietley down to sleep he sleepeth as saints sleep to awake in glory my dear husband you wanted to know a bout binghamton there is nothing particular only excitiments all the time there is not a great deal of work going on the weather is very unsettled now Mr Seberg of binghamton died the other day rather suden Mr riley as lived in fanimen house died quite suden Mr depue is dead this over two months Mr hensdale is been very sick ugene is in Nashville he is been gone about three weeks he rote to his father and said they was in a place called blu hell untill they would singh a paper to stay in the quartermaster department for 6 months he was a boughs rough that took them down there you wanted to know about the garden we have peas up we have a bed of onions up and nother one planted we have winter chabge seed up our strawberess look good they are notted for flower very full they currens bushes look better than you see they are very full of blom and the goseberey are full to allmost everbody is dead on account of the worm last year the large grape vine is dead but the little one is shotting good

Mr lindet cut it and i am afraid it killed it the large pear tree is nothing but a mass of blow and your green gage is full of blow but the washington is not in blow yet. we are planting potatoes we have two lots for potatoes the flower garden look very nice the roses look beatifull i have made up all the beds myself i miss you for planting the flowers for me i have a great many reeds up i have beatiful pannies i had a letter from Alfred the other and he says he is well off but he had not heard of you so i sent him your direction on a envilope so he can rite to you and i sent him word how you was Austin send his kindest love to his father he was glad to here that you was well and he says he is trying to do his best he can he is gone in to work this week so i hope we shall get along so dont worrey about us if you have a months pay you can send it if you like if you do send it by Admas express companey but if you dont want to draw any dont for us we shall get along good enought with out but if you have to have dont keep on your person for i am afraid you would lose it if would not be safe some one might rob you of it if you do send that rite me a letter so i may know i shall keep it till you come home little henery gives his love to his dear father and he send a
