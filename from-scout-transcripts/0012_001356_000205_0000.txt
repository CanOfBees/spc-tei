Binghamton May 22 my dear son It is with pleasure that i rite these few lines to you hoping they will you in good health in answere to your kind and welcome letter that i received from you May 20 and was glad to here that you was well as bless god it leaves us all well we are had bad colds but we are getting over them my dear boy you wanted to know if father had said in his letterswhen he cepted to come home he rote to me about two weeks ago and he said they expected to be discharged on the first of june and i hope you will come home with him and if you dont when you get your pay be sure get Mr Curtis to go with you to Actams express company offices and send your

money home to me it will not be safe for you to bring that money home yourself take out a enought for your ure to bring you home as you will get a pass to come home no doubt and then be sure send the rest home i will take good care of it for you there is so maney robereys ever day it makes one afraid i am rite for your father to do the same it will be the safes way it would be very hard for you to lose all you money after working so hard for it dont send it by the post there is so much lost send it by AAams express father was not very well when i heard from him Austin send his kind love to you and he was

dreadfull pleased with his ring and shield he was much obliged to you for them he long to see you he is counting of the time of you coming home Henery send his love to hattey and he cried because there was nothing for him and he hopes you will bring him some little shells and if you can bring me a couple of shells from the south i should like them work is not so dull after all at binghamton pat foley send his best respect to you pat is working for george how long with Austin this day i have a letter from your father and he says you are unkind for not answering his letters he is rote two letters and

you are not answering one of them besure my dear child rite to him i am afraid you are not had thes the direction is William Smith Chattenooga tenesse care S C Mearers Box 24 father says he shall come home in june he will ask for his time the first of june and he thinks he shall be home the middle of june and he say he shall call for you at Nashvill and i hope you will come with fathere if you can stop till then it will be the safesty way rite to me as soon as you can and send me word what you shall do i have sent you a few stamps about coming home So i must conclud good by god bless my dear boy a kis from me your mother Eliza smith take care of your blanket and bring it home and buy a nother if you can
