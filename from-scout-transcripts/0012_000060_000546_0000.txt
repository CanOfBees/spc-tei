Neosho, Mo Oct 29th Dear Aunt I received a card from Nimma about a week ago with the news of Grandmas death it was a shock to me I can only think of Grandma as she looked when I saw her last (when we lived in N.C.) pleasant & cheerful and ever ready to help me with my lesson I remember well the first letter I wrote to Grandma I was just nine years old, I kept a copy of it among my keepsakes a long time, I have all of the long good letters to me, I will miss them now since she is not here to write any more I have some letters from Grandpa that I prize very highly, I enjoyed talking with "the Soneir" about his

Page 3

visit to your house last simmer he as been here three times the past week to see if I had the Knoxville papers containing an account of Grandma's death, he put a short notice in his paper which I enclose, I hope my mental facilities may ne as active as hers, through the last hears of my life, you will miss Dear Grandma so sadly The children have been having chills this fall Eula's health is not good, they are all going to school have a chill one day & go to School the next, I give them quinin and break them up for a few days then they return, my own health is fine. I never have time to feel sick, you & uncle Mack come out here and spend awhile and I will

Page 4

go home with you, I am in earnest , I will go to Knoxville if you think I could get along there Sister Ellen has wanted Eula for a year or two but I never could quite make up my mind to let her go so far from me, but that will be a nice distance, I think the change of climate would do her good also, I have a great many kind friend here, but I think I would like to live near some of my relatives, Nimma wants me to go to Waco but I never liked any part of Texas and was not strong & well there as I am here, and Mr Jarnagins

family will help me educate the children Eula my very so I would rather be nearer them than farther away. it is time for the mail to go, write soon again you have my warmest sympathy in this Great Trial I too know what it is to part with loved ones, Love to Uncle Mack and Aunt May . Your Aff Niece Lizzie Jarnagin
