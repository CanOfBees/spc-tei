Nashville, Tenn., June 30th, 1865 My Dear Anna yours of the 24th came to hand on the 30th which was Sabbath Afternoon about two o clock I have mentioned in several letters that i have received 6 Dollars from you i wish you would mention it as soon as you get that Letter So you need not send any more at present I wish that they may be enough to do me until i come home Whenever that will be you wanted to know who was our General they tell me that Genl Thos is the head Genl which governs the state of Tennessee Alabama and Georgia and that send Doolittle is our genl and Genl Stoneman is the Commander of this state stationed at Knoxville they say that he has issued an order to muster us out it is our Colonel that has got us kept they make money doing nothing but going around a whoiinj but it may be that we may be mustered out quite unexpected to any of them Next Thursday is the Election day here What Wileys Mother was telling you I believe he had no notion of coming home on furlough he has to pay his fare and i think if he had any money to spare he would rather buy Whiskey. But the Second Lieut had applied for his Resignation and it was sent back because it was not properly drawn up so there was

a man stood by told me that the clerk was talking to him about it and he told him not to mind it that he would get home Just as soon without it So it is hard to tell how it will be The most of our Officers is sorry to see the was coming to a close they are so slow turning things in such as mules and Niggerrs and Juns They are Paying soldiers off every day they Paid all the Nigers last week There was a very sad affair Occured last Saturday Afternoon one of our guard had Arrested a Soldier belonging to the 188 Ohio i could not ascertain whether it was for drunkness or not but at anyrate he was takeing him to Jail and the report is that he tryed to run away when the guard hollerd him three times what he paid no attention to so he up with his gun and shot him so that he died in a few minutes afterwards the Poor fellow had been out in the service ever since the War broke out and was a fine man so says them in the same Camp with him Anna What you said about your teeth i think you had better get ready for to get a set of Artificial but i think that i would let cooper pull the stumps you know that chlorform is dangerous But it is very easy for me to tell you to get them and dont send you no money to do it with I hope that this will find you all well as it leaves me Yours as usual David
