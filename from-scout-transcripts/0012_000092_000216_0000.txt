In front of Atlanta Ga August 2 o nd n 64 Friend William I received your most welcolm and kind letter last evening and was pleased to hear that you wound was a getting well. I know you had an awful sevear wound and in a bad place.. we had heard that you were dead but I know better now..Conrad Ault died with his wound.. Sergent Hill and Carr were both wounded in the right arme. ..All the Boys in our company are well fat and hearty we draw full rashions of every thing ever sence we started on this campain and whiskey throught in well Bill we havend got Atlanta

Page 2

yet but we intend to have it and every thing that is in it and there army to boot. ..our men have commenced the seage and through a shell in to the city every five minuets day and night so you know that havend much peace in town... thay charged our works all a longe the line but thay coulde not come through. also we killed nearly half of there army be fore thay would stop but thay were so drunk that thay didend no what thay were at. .. Sherman sent a Division out of every corps to burie the dead but your Boys got so drunk on the rebs whiskey that thay diderd burey eveary rleble some rebles had as high a ten canteens on a carring whiskey to the front

.. well Bill I will try and get that money a pay day and do as you sed for me to do with it ..well Bill I will give you the names of the Boys that has killed on this campain from Reily. they are as follows. David Rees John Bresler David Bresler Conrad Hult Jeff Rolls ..the 95th Ohio and 97th Ind started afor home this morning .. the 69th is detached to the 3rd Brigade

Well Will I will bring my scribbling to a close by requesting you to rite soon and let me know how you are a gitting a longe with your wound I remane your most obedient Servent Southgate Cregmile Co. E. 69 O. Y. V. J 2ond Brigade 1st Division 74th A Co Atlanta Ga Rite soon
