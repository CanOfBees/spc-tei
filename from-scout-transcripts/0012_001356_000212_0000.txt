Binghamton March 25, 1865. My dear husband it is with pleasure i rite these few lines to you in answer to your kind letter that i received from you March 2d and was glad to here that your as well as you be i am sorry to here that you have got such a bad cold as it leaves us all at this time bless god for it well i hope your cold will be better in a few days i have writting two letters to you and your letter states you have not received none it is quite nrruses to know where they go to i hope you will receive this you wanted to know if i received the thirty Dollars that you sent me i received it quite safe March the 12 by Morgans express and i was so glad of it for austin was not at work he could not work we have had such awfull flood as never was known before in the memorey of man the two bridges both was taken away and the rail road bridge was not passible for day the water reached clear from the river to the swamp against us all one mass of water all the people had to move out of the houses the water reached up to Mr hindet house over the Sgueneatuk river all but one house it taken ever thing before it houses barns back house fences and logs and ever thing in reach of it

a great many people are left homeless i am sorry for them a great maney poor people have lost all they had owego was all under water and Elmira suffered severley and vestel was all under water and a greater part of union under water and rochester was all under water a great many pleope drowned beside the damage done clean from New hamshir clean Minnesota Sweping ever thing before it there was one man and team of horses came down the river the man was alive when he reache here and called for help but the horses was both dead and he struck against the bridge and was the last they see of him he went under know not how he was there was eight of binghamton men came on a little boat called a stemer worked with a camp the started from the asylum one was drowned a french man the rest was saved j h lesvise was one of them the was all drunk Mr king had to move up here the water was 2 foot depht in his house the water is 4 foot deep in the road now we have to goo through the pistol factor to get down town we could not pass there one a feern days ajae chushinys had to move out it is ruined his house he was entite out of coal i received your money on wensday and was gone to get some coal on thursday and the wartens rose in the night so i could not get any

we suffer here with cold we cannot get any coal up here wile the water is down out of the road the have duped a car of coal through the bridge and George Manene is got it and i am going to try to have him bring me some round the other road if he will else i wish you was at home my dear husband you could get all the work to do you wanted i hows long to see you my dear boy it will be a long teagers 6 month to me such a one as i have never seen before the thoughts of you going so much further i all most killes me i am afraid you go through more than you let me know my dear boy i greave for you i am not happey no more than you i hope we shall meet again with the blessings of god and live happy together my blessings goes with you were ever you and i hope the lord will bring you back to us again i blister the letter with my tear to think you cannot get one word from us at home my thoughts are daily with you and your dear little boy Henery is always talking about you and he wants you to come home he says if father was here he should not mind nothing Austin send his kind love to you and he is well and he hopes the time will soon come when you will come back for he says when you come back he will go down to alfred at Nashvill

my dear husband i hope you will rite to me often as i am soo glad to here from you o doo rite as often as you can alfred rote to austin and he received the letter the same day as i received yours he was well and he sent us word you was gone to Noxville that was a hundred miles from Nashville my dear husband i have had the house insured in the Glen falls company and i have the Noterey Austin is hoping round like little dadey fetching everthing in life you use he came over the feerey with a mater fruit and broom we have got the hams and they are nice pat foley send his best respects to you me and henery is been over the ferey three times Mr spencer is home and is got a bad cold he send his respects to you Mr clark came home Mr and Mrs hindet send there love to you Mr and Mrs king send there love to you besure rite as soon as you can rite with ink if you can get it you will have a nice bit of money when you come if you live to stop 6 months so that you wont have to work so hard when you com again so i must conclud with our kindes love to you Mr and Mrs Coley send there best respects to you your dear wife Eliza smith

Flood of March 1865Mr William SmithChattanogaTenn Box 27Care of SC Mearses
